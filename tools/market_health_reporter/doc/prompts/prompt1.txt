## Metric Reference

Use these definitions when analyzing the data. Reference specific metric keys from the dataset exactly.

### Volume-Volatility Correlation (`vvcorrelation`)
Measures the statistical relationship between trading volume and price volatility. In organic markets, high-volume periods correlate with increased volatility — genuine buying/selling pressure moves prices. Artificial volume (wash trading) inflates volume without a corresponding price impact, breaking this correlation.

- **Key:** `vvcorrelation`
- **Healthy range:** r > 0.5 sustained; values below 0.4 warrant investigation
- **Anomalous pattern:** Sustained correlation below 0.4 over multiple days, especially during periods of reported high volume
- **Severity thresholds:** r < 0.4 → MODERATE; r < 0.25 → HIGH; r < 0.1 sustained → CRITICAL
- **False-positive check:** A correlation drop coinciding with a market-wide shock (rapid BTC price move) is expected across all exchanges and is not exchange-specific evidence of manipulation.

### First-Digit Distribution (`firstdigitdist`)
Benford's Law predicts the frequency of leading digits in natural datasets: ~30.1% begin with 1, ~17.6% with 2, ~12.5% with 3, etc. Wash trading bots executing fixed-size orders produce round numbers, creating an overrepresentation of digits like 1 and 5, or underrepresentation of high digits depending on the bot's order sizing strategy.

- **Key:** `firstdigitdist`
- **Healthy pattern:** Distribution approximates Benford's Law (30.1%, 17.6%, 12.5%, 9.7%, 7.9%, 6.7%, 5.8%, 5.1%, 4.6% for digits 1–9)
- **Anomalous pattern:** Any digit deviating >5 percentage points from its Benford's expected frequency
- **Severity thresholds:** Max deviation 5–10pp → LOW; 10–15pp → MODERATE; >15pp → HIGH; multiple digits simultaneously >10pp deviation → CRITICAL
- **False-positive check:** Small `tradecount` values (<1000) reduce Benford's Law reliability. State sample size when assessing.

### Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test for First-Digit Distribution (`benfordlawtest`)
A formal statistical test comparing the empirical digit distribution to Benford's Law.

- **Key:** `benfordlawtest`
- **Critical value formula:** 1.36 / sqrt(`tradecount`)
- **Interpretation:** If test statistic > critical value → reject null hypothesis → data deviates from Benford's Law at the chosen significance level
- **Severity:** Rejection alone is MODERATE. Rejection + first-digit visual deviation + adequate sample size (>500) → HIGH. Add concurrent volume or time-of-trade anomaly → CRITICAL.
- **False-positive check:** The K-S test is highly sensitive to large sample sizes — with tradecount > 10,000, even minor deviations may produce rejection. Assess practical vs. statistical significance.

### Volume Distribution (`volumedist`)
Maps the frequency of different transaction sizes. Organic markets follow a power law: many small transactions, few large ones. The histogram shows a steep initial drop-off and a long right tail. Wash trading distorts this by introducing clusters of round-number, fixed-size orders.

- **Key:** `volumedist`
- **Healthy pattern:** Power law distribution — steep left side, long right tail, no pronounced clustering at specific size intervals
- **Anomalous pattern:** Unusual concentrations at specific transaction sizes; abnormally short tail (missing large trades); bimodal distribution
- **Severity:** Minor tail deviation → LOW; Clear clustering at round numbers → HIGH; Completely non-power-law distribution → CRITICAL

### Time-of-Trade (`timeoftrade`)
Measures trade frequency distribution across 60 minutes within an hour (or 60 seconds within a minute). Human traders trade unpredictably; bots often trade at regular intervals or at a single predictable time, creating either periodic spikes or unnaturally uniform distributions.

- **Key:** `timeoftrade`
- **Healthy pattern:** Irregular distribution with natural clustering around round minutes (0, 15, 30, 45) due to human psychology, but significant randomness
- **Anomalous patterns:**
  1. *Periodic spikes:* Trades concentrated at identical seconds/minutes across hours → strong bot signature
  2. *Uniform distribution:* Unusually even spread across all 60 intervals → bot spreading orders to avoid detection
- **Severity:** Mild regularity → LOW; Clear periodicity at specific intervals → HIGH; Perfect or near-perfect uniformity → CRITICAL

### Buy/Sell Ratio (`buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`)
The proportion of buy market orders to total orders. Balanced markets cluster around 0.5. Sustained deviations indicate either genuine directional pressure or artificial order flow.

- **Keys:** `buysellratio` (normalized), `buysellratioabs` (absolute counts)
- **Healthy range:** 0.4–0.6 under normal conditions; short deviations during trending markets are expected
- **Anomalous patterns:**
  1. *Sustained extreme ratio:* Sustained above 0.7 or below 0.3 outside strong trend periods
  2. *Unnatural steadiness:* Ratio locked at a specific value with unusually low variance during high-volatility periods — suggests coordinated order placement
- **Severity:** Short deviation (1–2 days) from 0.4–0.6 → LOW; Sustained (>3 days) outside 0.35–0.65 → MODERATE; Sustained outside 0.3–0.7 with concurrent volatility → HIGH; Locked ratio during significant price movement → CRITICAL

### Volume Weighted Average Price (`vwap`)
Calculates the average price weighted by volume. Significant divergence between VWAP and spot price indicates that large-volume trades are occurring at price levels substantially different from the market consensus — a potential signal of coordinated price support or suppression.

- **Key:** `vwap`
- **Healthy pattern:** VWAP tracks within normal trading range; divergences are temporary and self-correcting
- **Anomalous patterns:**
  1. *Persistent support:* VWAP consistently above spot price during declining markets (possible buy-side support)
  2. *Persistent suppression:* VWAP consistently below spot price during rising markets (possible sell-side pressure)
- **Severity:** Minor temporal divergence → LOW; Consistent directional divergence over >3 days → MODERATE; Extreme divergence (>10% sustained) concurrent with abnormal buy/sell ratio → HIGH
- **False-positive check:** Stablecoin trading pairs have inherently compressed price ranges, making VWAP divergence metrics less informative. Low-liquidity tokens may show VWAP distortions due to a small number of large trades.

## Cross-Metric Correlation Pairs (Ordered by Diagnostic Weight)

When multiple metrics show concurrent anomalies in the same time window, manipulation confidence increases substantially. Use this matrix:

| Metric Pair | Combined Signal | Manipulation Type |
|---|---|---|
| First-digit deviation + K-S rejection | **Strong** (adjust confidence +25) | Wash trading |
| Volume distribution deviation + low vvcorrelation | **Strong** (adjust confidence +25) | Artificial volume |
| Time-of-trade regularity + abnormal buy/sell ratio | **Moderate** (adjust confidence +15) | Automated bot trading |
| VWAP divergence + abnormal buy/sell ratio | **Moderate** (adjust confidence +15) | Price support/suppression |
| Any single metric in isolation | **Weak** (confidence ≤25) | Insufficient for claim |
| All metrics clean (no anomaly ≥MODERATE) | **No signal** | Likely clean market |

## Article Output Requirements

Write the article between `<article>` and `</article>` tags. Structure:

**Frontmatter (required):**
```
---
date: 'YYYY-MM-DD — YYYY-MM-DD'
entities: 'Exchange, Token1, Token2'
title: 'Descriptive title referencing the exchange and finding'
---
```

**Opening summary:** 3–6 numbered bullets. Each bullet = one key finding with the metric name, observed value, and benchmark comparison. Final bullet = confidence score statement.

**Metric sections:** One section per anomalous metric (MODERATE severity or above only). Structure each section as:
  - State the metric and its observed value
  - Compare to the expected benchmark
  - Describe the temporal pattern (onset, duration, resolution)
  - Identify any corroborating metrics in the same window
  - Avoid speculation beyond what the data supports

**Correlation analysis section** (if multiple anomalies present): Describe the concurrent pattern and what the combination of signals indicates.

**Conclusion:** Summarize the overall confidence assessment, state what the evidence supports and what it does not, and note any unresolved questions.
