## Analysis Procedure

Follow these steps in order:

### Step 1: Initial Metric Scan
Examine all metrics in the provided data. For each metric, compute whether it falls within or outside its benchmark range.

### Step 2: Anomaly Detection
Flag metrics that breach their thresholds. Rank anomalies by severity (how far they deviate from benchmarks).

### Step 3: Cross-Metric Correlation
Check if flagged anomalies occurred concurrently. Concurrent anomalies across multiple metrics strengthen the case for manipulation.

### Step 4: Report Construction
Lead with the most severe anomaly. Build the narrative by connecting related metrics. If no significant anomalies exist, state this clearly with supporting evidence.

## Metric Reference

### Volume-Volatility Correlation (`vvcorrelation`)
Measures the relationship between trading volume and price volatility over time.
- **Benchmark:** Correlation ≥ 0.4 is normal. Real trades move prices.
- **Anomaly:** Correlation consistently < 0.4 over extended periods → artificial volume (trades that don't impact price).

### First-Digit Distribution (`firstdigitdist`)
Tests whether leading digits of trading data conform to Benford's Law.
- **Benchmark:** Digit frequencies should approximate Benford's distribution (digit 1 ≈ 30.1%, digit 9 ≈ 4.6%).
- **Anomaly:** Disproportionate frequency of higher leading digits (8, 9) or uniform distribution → possible wash trading or fabricated data.

### Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test (`benfordlawtest`)
Statistical test comparing the observed first-digit distribution against Benford's Law.
- **Threshold calculation:** critical_value = 1.36 / sqrt(`tradecount`)
- **Interpretation:**
  - test_value > critical_value → reject null hypothesis → data likely does NOT conform to Benford's Law (suspicious)
  - test_value ≤ critical_value → insufficient evidence of deviation (normal)
- **Important:** Always report both the test value and the critical value with the trade count.

### Volume Distribution (`volumedist`)
Shows the frequency distribution of transaction sizes.
- **Benchmark:** Should follow a power law — many small trades, few large trades. The histogram shows steep drop-off for small values with a long right tail.
- **Anomaly:** Deviation from power law, especially: unusually high concentration of same-size trades, absence of small/medium trades, or excessive large trades.

### Time-of-Trade (`timeoftrade`)
Analyzes trade execution timing within each minute (60 buckets, 0–59 seconds).
- **Benchmark:** Human traders create variable, somewhat clustered patterns.
- **Anomaly (two-sided):**
  - Extreme clustering: high frequency at specific seconds → scheduled bot activity
  - Extreme uniformity: nearly equal counts across all seconds → algorithmic volume generation

### Buy/Sell Ratio (`buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`)
Proportion of buy vs. sell market orders per time period.
- **Benchmark:** 0.4–0.6 range indicates balanced market activity.
- **Anomaly:**
  - Ratio consistently outside 0.4–0.6 → directional pressure
  - Unnaturally steady ratio during high volatility → automated systems maintaining artificial balance
- **Context matters:** Both volatile swings AND artificial steadiness can indicate manipulation.

### Volume Weighted Average Price (`vwap`)
Average price weighted by volume over a time period.
- **Benchmark:** VWAP should track close to actual trading prices.
- **Anomaly:** Persistent, significant divergence between VWAP and market price → possible price manipulation through strategic order placement.

---

## Cross-Metric Anomaly Patterns

When multiple metrics are anomalous simultaneously, the following combinations are especially indicative:

1. **Volume Distribution + Volume-Volatility Correlation:** Non-power-law distribution with low VV correlation → large artificial trades that don't move prices.
2. **First-Digit Distribution + K-S Test:** Both flagged simultaneously → strong evidence of data fabrication (wash trading).
3. **VWAP + Buy/Sell Ratio:** VWAP divergence with abnormal buy/sell ratio → coordinated price manipulation.
4. **Time-of-Trade + Buy/Sell Ratio:** Uniform/clustered timing with ratio outside 0.4–0.6 → automated trading systems influencing sentiment.
