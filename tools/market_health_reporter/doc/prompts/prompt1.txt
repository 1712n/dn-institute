# Procedure

Follow these four steps in order. Reason through them step by step before writing the article.

1. **Inspect the data.** The market data is supplied at the end of this prompt inside `<data>` tags. Identify which of the metrics defined in the Metric reference below show anomalous values, and over which timestamps. List the anomalies internally before drafting any prose.
2. **Decide whether anomalies are present.** If at least one metric breaches its anomalous-pattern threshold described in the Metric reference, classify the period as anomalous and proceed to step 3. If no metric breaches its threshold, classify the period as clean and use the "no anomaly" output template at the bottom.
3. **Construct the narrative.** Open with the metric that shows the largest deviation. Cross-reference any other metrics that deviated in the same direction or at the same timestamps (use the "combined-anomaly patterns" section as a guide). Tie each finding to a specific timestamp range and the underlying numeric value.
4. **Format the article.** Use the output template below. Frontmatter goes between two `---` lines. The article body uses a `## Summary` section followed by a `## Metrics used` section with one `###` subsection per metric that contributed to the finding. Wrap the entire article in `<article>` and `</article>` tags.

# Metric reference

For each metric: data key, anomalous pattern, and benchmark.

| Metric | Data key(s) | Anomalous pattern | Benchmark |
|---|---|---|---|
| Volume-Volatility Correlation | `vvcorrelation` | Coefficient consistently below 0.4 over extended periods, suggesting trading volume not correlated with price movement (artificial volume). | Normal range varies by market; below 0.4 sustained is suspicious. |
| First-Digit Distribution | `firstdigitdist` | Significant deviation from Benford's Law in leading digits of trade values (e.g. disproportionate high-leading-digits like 8 or 9, or compressed distributions). | Empirical first-digit distribution typically converges to Benford's Law. |
| K-S Test for First-Digit Distribution | `benfordlawtest`, `tradecount` | `benfordlawtest > 1.36 / sqrt(tradecount)`. The right-hand side is the critical value at the chosen significance level; values above this reject conformity to Benford's Law. | Test value at or below the critical value means insufficient evidence of deviation. |
| Volume Distribution | `volumedist` | Histogram diverges from a power-law shape (heavy concentration of large "whale" trades, missing small or medium retail trades, or symmetric and flat distributions). | Power law: many small trades, few large; asymmetric, long right tail. |
| Time-of-trade | `timeoftrade` | Either heavy spikes at specific seconds or minutes within the hour, or near-uniform distribution across all 60 buckets. Both indicate automated trading. | Genuine human trading is unevenly clustered but not periodic. |
| Buy/Sell Ratio | `buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs` | Values outside 0.4-0.6, or unnatural stability in this band during periods of high volatility. | Healthy markets sit in 0.4-0.6 with natural noise. |
| Volume Weighted Average Price | `vwap` | Sustained large gap between VWAP and the actual trading price beyond normal trading-range fluctuations. | No fixed numeric threshold; large persistent divergences are suspicious. |

# Combined-anomaly patterns

When multiple metrics deviate together they corroborate manipulation. Cite combinations explicitly when present.

- **Volume Distribution + VV Correlation:** Volume-distribution divergence from power law (especially with many large trades) plus low VV correlation indicates manipulated volume. Large trades should drive volatility; if they do not, the volume is likely artificial.
- **First-Digit Distribution + K-S Test:** Both metrics deviating together is strong evidence of fabricated trade data, consistent with wash trading.
- **VWAP + Buy/Sell Ratio:** A persistent VWAP-price gap combined with an unbalanced buy/sell ratio is consistent with directional price manipulation through one-sided order flow.
- **Time-of-trade + Buy/Sell Ratio:** Periodic Time-of-trade plus an off-norm buy/sell ratio indicates an automated system attempting to influence price or sentiment.
