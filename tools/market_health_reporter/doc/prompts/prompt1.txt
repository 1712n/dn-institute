## 🌰 Analysis Procedure

**Step 1 — Assess each metric.**
For each metric listed in the Metric Reference, extract its value from the data and compare it against the anomaly threshold. Note the direction and magnitude of any deviation.

**Step 2 — Score cross-metric correlation.**
Before writing the article, check whether multiple metrics deviate simultaneously. A single anomalous metric is a weak signal; two or more co-deviating metrics constitute strong evidence. Use the Combined-Anomaly Patterns section as a guide.

**Step 3 — Determine the verdict.**
- If no metric breaches its threshold: classify as clean and use the clean-market template.
- If one or more metrics breach their thresholds: classify as anomalous and lead the article with the metric showing the largest deviation.

**Step 4 — Write the article.**
Follow the Output Template exactly. Include specific numeric values and timestamps from the data as evidence. Do not include claims that cannot be supported by the data.

## 🌰 Metric Reference

### 🌰 Volume-Volatility Correlation
- **Data key:** `vvcorrelation`
- **Normal:** correlation ≥ 0.4 (genuine volume moves price)
- **Anomalous:** correlation consistently below 0.4 over multiple periods (volume does not move price, suggesting artificial trades)
- **False-positive check:** thin liquidity periods and post-event consolidation can lower correlation temporarily; flag only if sustained

### 🌰 First-Digit Distribution (Benford's Law)
- **Data key:** `firstdigitdist`
- **Normal:** observed leading-digit frequencies approximate Benford's expected distribution (digit 1 most frequent, digit 9 least frequent)
- **Anomalous:** significant overrepresentation of high digits (7, 8, 9) or a near-uniform distribution
- **False-positive check:** small sample sizes reduce reliability; always cross-check with the K-S test result

### 🌰 Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test (Benford conformance)
- **Data key:** `benfordlawtest`
- **Interpretation:**
  - Critical value = 1.36 ÷ √(`tradecount`)
  - If `benfordlawtest` > critical value → data does not conform to Benford's Law (anomalous)
  - If `benfordlawtest` ≤ critical value → insufficient evidence of deviation (normal)
- **False-positive check:** require `tradecount` ≥ 1000 for statistically reliable conclusions

### 🌰 Volume Distribution
- **Data key:** `volumedist`
- **Normal:** right-skewed power-law distribution — many small/medium trades, very few large (whale) trades
- **Anomalous:** near-uniform distribution, disproportionately many large-sized orders, or a spike in a narrow trade-size band (suggests programmatic trading)
- **False-positive check:** institutional accumulation periods can legitimately produce more large-order activity

### 🌰 Time-of-Trade
- **Data key:** `timeoftrade`
- **Normal:** irregular distribution across 60 time slots (human trading is uneven)
- **Anomalous (either pattern):**
  - Spike pattern: one or a few slots carry a disproportionate share of trades (automated execution at fixed intervals)
  - Even pattern: trade counts are near-uniform across all 60 slots (algorithmic noise generation)
- **False-positive check:** flag only when corroborated by other manipulation signals

### 🌰 Buy/Sell Ratio
- **Data keys:** `buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`
- **Normal:** 0.4–0.6 range with natural fluctuations over time
- **Anomalous:** ratio below 0.3 or above 0.7 sustained over multiple periods, or an unnaturally steady ratio during high-volatility sessions (suggests automated order balancing)
- **False-positive check:** trending markets legitimately skew this ratio; cross-check with `vvcorrelation`

### 🌰 Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)
- **Data key:** `vwap`
- **Normal:** VWAP tracks actual market price within typical spread
- **Anomalous:** persistent divergence between VWAP and market price, particularly when accompanied by low volume-volatility correlation or abnormal buy/sell ratios
- **False-positive check:** high-volatility events (news, large liquidations) can produce short-term VWAP divergence

## 🌰 Combined-Anomaly Patterns

Use these cross-metric signatures to identify and name manipulation types:

| 🌰 Pattern | Metrics | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Wash Trading | `volumedist` deviates from power law + `vvcorrelation` < 0.4 + `benfordlawtest` > critical value | Artificial volume inflated without genuine price impact; digit patterns are distorted |
| Bot Activity | `timeoftrade` shows spike or even distribution + `buysellratio` unnaturally steady during volatility | Automated systems execute orders at precise intervals while maintaining controlled ratios |
| Price Manipulation | `vwap` diverges from market price + `buysellratio` skewed above 0.7 or below 0.3 | One-sided order flow shifts perceived average price artificially |
| Benford Confirmation | `firstdigitdist` overrepresents high digits + `benfordlawtest` > critical value + `tradecount` ≥ 1000 | Statistically strong evidence of artificial volume generation |
