## Procedure

1. Evaluate each metric against its threshold. Assign a severity: NORMAL / CAUTION / SUSPICIOUS / ANOMALOUS.
2. Check cross-metric patterns (see Correlation Patterns below) to confirm or downgrade individual signals.
3. Rule out false positives using the checklist below.
4. Write the article starting with the strongest anomaly. If no anomalies exist, report a clean assessment with evidence.

## Metric Reference Cards

### Volume-Volatility Correlation
- Key: `vvcorrelation`
- Normal: ≥ 0.4
- Caution: 0.3–0.4
- Suspicious: 0.2–0.3
- Anomalous: < 0.2
- Meaning: Real trades move price. Consistently low correlation suggests artificial volume that does not impact price discovery.

### First-Digit Distribution
- Key: `firstdigitdist`
- Normal: Empirical distribution converges with Benford's Law (digit 1 ≈ 30%, digit 9 ≈ 4.6%).
- Anomalous: Disproportionate frequency of higher digits (7, 8, 9) or uniform distribution across all digits.
- Meaning: Non-conformity suggests manufactured trade data, potential wash trading or coordinated sell-offs.

### Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test (Benford's Law)
- Key: `benfordlawtest`
- Trade count key: `tradecount`
- Critical value: 1.36 / √(tradecount)
- Normal: test value ≤ critical value (fail to reject null hypothesis — data conforms to Benford's Law).
- Anomalous: test value > critical value (reject null hypothesis — data deviates from Benford's Law).
- Note: K-S test is sensitive to sample size. Always report both test value and critical value with the trade count.

### Volume Distribution
- Key: `volumedist`
- Normal: Follows power law — many small/medium trades, few large "whale" trades. Histogram shows steep initial drop-off with a long right tail.
- Anomalous: Deviation from power law, such as unusually high concentration of large trades, absence of small/medium trades, or symmetric distribution.
- Meaning: Power law violation suggests coordinated large-order activity inconsistent with organic retail trading.

### Time-of-Trade
- Key: `timeoftrade`
- Normal: Irregular, human-like distribution across seconds/minutes with natural clustering around certain periods.
- Anomalous (two-sided):
  - Spikes: High frequency at specific seconds/minutes (e.g., trades clustering at :00, :30).
  - Flat: Nearly uniform distribution across all 60 seconds/minutes.
- Meaning: Both patterns indicate automated trading systems or bot activity rather than human traders.

### Buy/Sell Ratio
- Key: `buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`
- Normal: 0.4–0.6 range with natural fluctuation.
- Caution: 0.3–0.4 or 0.6–0.7.
- Anomalous: < 0.3 or > 0.7, OR unnatural steadiness (low variance) during high-volatility periods.
- Meaning: Extreme ratios suggest directional pressure. Steady ratios during volatile conditions suggest automated market manipulation.

### Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)
- Key: `vwap`
- Normal: VWAP tracks close to actual trading price with minor deviations.
- Anomalous: Consistent, significant divergence between VWAP and market price over extended periods.
- Meaning: Persistent divergence may indicate that large hidden orders are skewing the perceived average price.

## Cross-Metric Correlation Patterns

| Pattern Name | Metrics Involved | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Wash Trading | Volume distribution deviates from power law + VV correlation < 0.3 + K-S test rejects Benford's | Large artificial trades that fail to move price and produce unnatural digit patterns |
| Bot Activity | Time-of-trade shows spikes or flat distribution + Buy/Sell ratio is unnaturally steady | Automated systems executing trades at programmatic intervals |
| Price Manipulation | VWAP diverges from market price + Buy/Sell ratio is extreme (< 0.3 or > 0.7) | Strategic buying/selling to distort perceived average price |
| Benford Confirmation | First-digit distribution deviates + K-S test value > critical value | Statistical confirmation that trade data does not follow expected natural patterns |

When two or more metrics from the same pattern are flagged, escalate the overall severity by one level.

## False-Positive Checklist

Before classifying any metric as SUSPICIOUS or ANOMALOUS, rule out:
- Market-wide events (e.g., major news, exchange listing, protocol upgrade) that explain unusual volume or ratio shifts.
- Low sample size (`tradecount` < 1000) making K-S test unreliable.
- Exchange promotions (zero-fee trading periods) that naturally inflate volume without manipulation.
- Stablecoin pair behavior (stablecoin pairs inherently have low volatility, producing low VV correlation without manipulation).
- Time zone effects (trading spikes during market open in major regions: UTC 0–2 for Asia, 13–15 for US).

If a benign explanation exists, downgrade severity and note the reasoning.

## Article Output Format

Create an article with your analysis. Place it inside `<article>` `</article>` tags.

Required front matter fields between `---` markers:
- `date`: Format `YYYY-MM-DD — YYYY-MM-DD` (analysis period start — end). Example: `'2023-10-02 — 2023-10-08'`
- `entities`: Exchanges and tokens implicated. Example: `'Huobi, HT, TRX, DOGE'`
- `title`: Descriptive title. Example: `'Uncovering Wash Trading and Market Manipulation on Huobi'`

Article structure:
1. **Summary**: 3–5 numbered bullet points with key findings and severity levels.
2. **Metric sections**: One subsection per metric analyzed. Each must include:
   - The metric value and its threshold comparison.
   - Severity classification (NORMAL / CAUTION / SUSPICIOUS / ANOMALOUS).
   - What the finding means in plain language.
   - Figure placeholder where applicable.
3. **Cross-metric analysis**: Identify which correlation patterns (if any) are triggered.
4. **Conclusion**: Overall assessment with confidence level.

Use ONLY data from the provided dataset. Follow the structure shown in the `<example>` tags.
