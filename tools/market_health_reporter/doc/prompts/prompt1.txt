## Analysis Methodology

### Step 1: Individual Metric Assessment

Evaluate each metric independently. For each one, determine whether the observed values fall within normal ranges or exhibit anomalous patterns.

### Step 2: Cross-Metric Correlation

After individual assessment, identify combinations of anomalous metrics that reinforce manipulation hypotheses:

- **Volume Distribution + Volume-Volatility Correlation**: A volume distribution deviating from power law (especially unusual concentration of large trades) combined with low volume-volatility correlation suggests artificial volume generation. Genuine large trades should introduce volatility; their absence alongside volume spikes indicates wash trading.
- **First-Digit Distribution + K-S Test**: Significant deviation from Benford's Law confirmed by a K-S test statistic exceeding the critical value provides strong statistical evidence of data manipulation, consistent with wash trading or coordinated artificial order placement.
- **VWAP + Buy/Sell Ratio**: Substantial divergence between VWAP and market price alongside abnormal buy/sell ratios suggests price manipulation through strategic order placement designed to influence the perceived average price.
- **Time-of-Trade + Buy/Sell Ratio**: High-frequency trades at identical timestamps or unnaturally uniform trade distribution across time intervals, combined with buy/sell ratio anomalies, indicate automated trading systems attempting to influence market prices or fabricate market sentiment.

### Step 3: Severity Classification

Classify overall findings using these severity levels:
- **Critical**: Multiple metrics show strong, persistent anomalies with clear cross-metric correlation. High confidence of deliberate manipulation.
- **High**: Two or more metrics show significant anomalies with some cross-correlation. Probable manipulation requiring further investigation.
- **Moderate**: Isolated metric anomalies or brief deviations that may indicate opportunistic manipulation or market stress events.
- **Low / No Evidence**: Metrics fall within expected ranges. No significant evidence of manipulation detected.

### Step 4: False Positive Awareness

Before concluding manipulation, consider alternative explanations:
- Market-wide events (exchange outages, major news, liquidation cascades) can produce anomalous patterns without manipulation.
- Low-liquidity pairs naturally exhibit higher variance in all metrics.
- Legitimate algorithmic market-making can produce time-of-trade patterns similar to wash trading.
Rule: Only flag manipulation when the evidence is consistent across multiple independent metrics and alternative explanations are insufficient.

## Metric Definitions and Thresholds

### Volume-Volatility Correlation
- **Data key**: `vvcorrelation`
- **Description**: Measures the statistical relationship between trading volume and price volatility over time. In healthy markets, increased volume correlates with increased volatility.
- **Anomalous pattern**: Consistently low correlation (significantly below 0.4) over extended periods, suggesting artificial volume that does not impact price discovery.
- **Benchmark**: Correlation coefficient persistently below 0.4 is suspicious. Observe trends over multiple time windows rather than individual snapshots.

### First-Digit Distribution
- **Data key**: `firstdigitdist`
- **Description**: Tests whether the leading digits of trade sizes conform to Benford's Law, a statistical expectation for naturally occurring numerical data.
- **Anomalous pattern**: Disproportionate frequency of higher leading digits (6-9) relative to lower digits (1-3), or an unnaturally uniform distribution across all digits.
- **Benchmark**: Natural trading data converges with Benford's Law distribution (digit 1 appears ~30.1%, digit 2 ~17.6%, etc.).

### Kolmogorov-Smirnov Test (Benford's Law)
- **Data key**: `benfordlawtest`
- **Description**: Quantifies the maximum deviation between the observed first-digit distribution and Benford's Law theoretical distribution.
- **Interpretation**: Compare the test statistic against the critical value. The critical value equals 1.36 divided by the square root of the sample size (stored in `tradecount`).
  - Test value > critical value → Reject null hypothesis. Data does NOT conform to Benford's Law (evidence of manipulation).
  - Test value ≤ critical value → Do not reject. Insufficient evidence of deviation from Benford's Law.

### Volume Distribution
- **Data key**: `volumedist`
- **Description**: Shows the frequency distribution of transaction sizes. Healthy markets follow a power law distribution where small/medium trades are frequent and large trades are rare.
- **Anomalous pattern**: Deviation from power law, such as unusual concentration of identically-sized trades, absence of small retail trades, or disproportionate whale activity.
- **Benchmark**: The histogram should show a steep initial drop-off with a long right tail. Symmetric or multi-modal distributions are suspicious.

### Time-of-Trade
- **Data key**: `timeoftrade`
- **Description**: Aggregates trade execution timestamps into 60 bins (one per second or minute), revealing temporal patterns in trading activity independent of the specific hour or day.
- **Anomalous pattern**: This metric flags two distinct patterns: (1) concentrated spikes showing high trade frequency at specific seconds/minutes, and (2) unnaturally uniform distribution across all 60 bins. Both patterns indicate bot activity or automated trading systems.
- **Benchmark**: Human trading naturally clusters around certain periods and shows irregular patterns. Perfect uniformity or precise periodicity is suspect.

### Buy/Sell Ratio
- **Data keys**: `buysellratio` and `buysellratioabs`
- **Description**: The proportion of buy versus sell market orders in each time period.
- **Anomalous pattern**: Ratios consistently outside the 0.4-0.6 range, or unnatural steadiness (low variance) during periods of high volatility, suggesting automated price management.
- **Benchmark**: A balanced market fluctuates around 0.5 with natural variance. Persistent stability in this metric, especially during volatile periods, indicates deliberate control.

### Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP)
- **Data key**: `vwap`
- **Description**: The average price weighted by trading volume, giving more weight to price levels with heavy activity.
- **Anomalous pattern**: Consistent and significant divergence between VWAP and actual market price, indicating that high-volume trades occur at atypical price levels.
- **Benchmark**: No fixed numerical threshold, but persistent deviation beyond normal trading fluctuations warrants investigation.
