## Analysis Methodology

Work through the metrics in order of diagnostic importance. For each metric, compare observed values against the benchmarks below and note any anomalies.

### Step 1: Volume-Volatility Correlation (`vvcorrelation`)
- **What it measures:** Relationship between trading volume and price volatility over time.
- **Red flag:** Correlation consistently below 0.4 over extended periods suggests artificial volume, since genuine trades typically move prices.
- **Benchmark:** Healthy markets show moderate-to-strong positive correlation (above 0.4).

### Step 2: First-Digit Distribution (`firstdigitdist`) + Benford's Law Test (`benfordlawtest`)
- **What it measures:** Whether leading digits in trading data follow Benford's Law, which natural datasets obey.
- **Red flag:** Disproportionate frequency of higher leading digits (7, 8, 9) instead of lower ones (1, 2, 3), or K-S test value exceeding the critical value.
- **K-S critical value formula:** `1.36 / sqrt(tradecount)`. If test value > critical value, the data likely deviates from Benford's Law, suggesting manipulation (e.g., wash trading).

### Step 3: Volume Distribution (`volumedist`)
- **What it measures:** Frequency of different transaction sizes.
- **Red flag:** Deviation from the expected power law distribution — e.g., unusually many large trades, absence of small/medium trades, or symmetric rather than right-skewed histograms.
- **Benchmark:** Normal markets follow a power law: many small retail trades, few large "whale" trades. The histogram should show a steep initial drop-off with a long tail.

### Step 4: Time-of-Trade (`timeoftrade`)
- **What it measures:** Distribution of trades across seconds/minutes within each time period.
- **Red flag (two-sided):** Either (a) high concentration of trades at identical timestamps, or (b) suspiciously even distribution across all seconds/minutes. Both patterns indicate bot activity.
- **Benchmark:** Human trading produces irregular, clustered patterns — not perfectly uniform or perfectly concentrated.

### Step 5: Buy/Sell Ratio (`buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`)
- **What it measures:** Proportion of buy vs. sell market orders over time.
- **Red flag:** Ratio consistently outside the 0.4–0.6 range, or unnaturally stable during high-volatility periods.
- **Benchmark:** Balanced markets hover around 0.5. Values significantly above 0.5 suggest buying pressure (potential price inflation); below 0.5 suggests selling pressure.

### Step 6: VWAP (`vwap`)
- **What it measures:** Volume-weighted average price — gives more weight to price levels with high trading activity.
- **Red flag:** Persistent, significant divergence between VWAP and actual trading price.
- **Benchmark:** In healthy markets, trading price tracks VWAP closely.

## Cross-Metric Patterns

When multiple anomalies appear simultaneously, the evidence for manipulation strengthens:

| Combination | Implication |
|---|---|
| Abnormal volume distribution + low volume-volatility correlation | Large trades not affecting price → likely artificial volume |
| Benford's Law deviation + high K-S test value | Strong statistical evidence of fabricated trading data |
| VWAP divergence + abnormal buy/sell ratio | Possible price manipulation through strategic order placement |
| Uniform time-of-trade + abnormal buy/sell ratio | Automated systems manipulating market sentiment |
