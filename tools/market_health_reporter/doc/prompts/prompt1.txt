## 🌰 PROCEDURE

Analyze the provided market data following these steps in order:

1. **Scan each metric** against its benchmark (see METRICS below). Flag values that cross anomalous thresholds.
2. **Identify anomaly clusters** — metrics that deviate in the same time window. Check for cross-metric patterns (see CROSS-METRIC PATTERNS).
3. **Build the narrative** starting from the strongest anomaly. Include specific timestamps and values as evidence for every claim.
4. **If data appears clean**, state this clearly. Briefly note which metrics were examined and why they fall within normal ranges. Do not fabricate concerns.

## 🌰 METRICS REFERENCE

Each metric below is keyed as shown. When analyzing, compare observed values against the benchmark.

### Volume-Volatility Correlation
- **Key:** `vvcorrelation`
- **What it measures:** Relationship between trading volume and price volatility over time.
- **Anomalous pattern:** Correlation consistently below 0.4 over extended periods — suggests artificial volume not moving prices.
- **Benchmark:** Correlation coefficient ≥ 0.4 is normal; sustained values below 0.4 are suspicious.

### First-Digit Distribution
- **Key:** `firstdigitdist`
- **What it measures:** Whether leading digits of trade sizes follow Benford's Law.
- **Anomalous pattern:** Disproportionate frequency of higher leading digits (8, 9) instead of lower ones (1, 2, 3).
- **Benchmark:** Empirical distribution should converge with Benford's Law.

### K-S Test (Benford's Law)
- **Key:** `benfordlawtest` · Sample size in `tradecount`
- **What it measures:** Statistical test comparing trade data distribution against Benford's Law.
- **Anomalous pattern:** Test value > critical value → reject null hypothesis → data does not conform.
- **Benchmark:** Critical value = 1.36 / √(`tradecount`). If test value ≤ critical value, no evidence of deviation.

### Volume Distribution
- **Key:** `volumedist`
- **What it measures:** Frequency of different transaction sizes (histogram).
- **Anomalous pattern:** Deviation from power-law — unusually many large trades or absence of small/medium trades.
- **Benchmark:** Expect power-law shape: steep drop-off at small values, long tail for large values. Small retail trades frequent, whale orders rare.

### Time-of-Trade
- **Key:** `timeoftrade`
- **What it measures:** Distribution of trades across seconds/minutes within each period (60-bucket array).
- **Anomalous pattern:** Either (a) sharp spikes at specific seconds/minutes, or (b) unnaturally even distribution across all buckets. Both suggest bot activity.
- **Benchmark:** No fixed threshold; flag patterns that deviate significantly from typical human trading behavior.

### Buy/Sell Ratio
- **Key:** `buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs`
- **What it measures:** Proportion of buy vs. sell market orders per period.
- **Anomalous pattern:** Ratio outside 0.4–0.6 range, or unnatural steadiness during high-volatility periods.
- **Benchmark:** Balanced market: 0.4–0.6. Above 0.5 = buying bias (price increase pressure). Below 0.5 = selling bias (price decrease pressure). Both irregular swings and artificial steadiness can indicate automation.

### VWAP
- **Key:** `vwap`
- **What it measures:** Volume-weighted average price — average price weighted by trading volume.
- **Anomalous pattern:** Significant, sustained divergence between VWAP and actual trading price.
- **Benchmark:** No fixed threshold; deviation beyond normal trading fluctuations is a red flag.

## 🌰 CROSS-METRIC PATTERNS

When anomalies appear simultaneously, check for these named signals:

**Wash Trading Signal:** Volume distribution breaks power-law + low volume-volatility correlation. Large trades that don't move prices suggest artificial volume.

**Benford Confirmation:** First-digit distribution deviates from Benford's Law + K-S test rejects null hypothesis. Together they strongly indicate fabricated trade data.

**Price Manipulation Signal:** VWAP diverges from market price + buy/sell ratio outside 0.4–0.6. Suggests strategic buying/selling to influence perceived average price.

**Bot Activity Signal:** Time-of-trade shows periodic spikes or flat distribution + buy/sell ratio abnormally steady. Indicates automated trading systems at work.
