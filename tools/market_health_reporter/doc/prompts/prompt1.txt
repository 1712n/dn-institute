### Analysis Workflow

**Step 1: Temporal Analysis**
Observe all metrics over time to establish baseline patterns and identify deviations.

**Step 2: Anomaly Detection**
Identify deviations exceeding the thresholds defined below.

**Step 3: Correlation Check**
Determine if multiple metrics show simultaneous anomalies (stronger evidence).

**Step 4: Report Construction**
- Start with the most significant deviation
- Include specific timestamps and quantitative values
- Only report if clear anomalous patterns exist

### Metric Definitions & Thresholds

| Metric | Key | Description | Anomalous Pattern | Benchmark |
|--------|-----|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Volume-Volatility Correlation** | `vvcorrelation` | Statistical relationship between trading volume and price volatility | Consistently < 0.4 over extended periods | Normal: varies; < 0.4 suspicious |
| **First-Digit Distribution** | `firstdigitdist` | Leading digit distribution compared to Benford's Law | Significant deviation (e.g., excess of 8s, 9s vs 1s, 2s, 3s) | Should conform to Benford's Law |
| **Benford K-S Test** | `benfordlawtest` | Kolmogorov-Smirnov test comparing data to Benford's Law | Test value > critical value | Critical value = 1.36 / √`tradecount` |
| **Volume Distribution** | `volumedist` | Frequency of transaction sizes | Deviation from power law (excess whale trades, missing retail trades) | Power law: many small, few large trades |
| **Time-of-Trade** | `timeoftrade` | Trade timing distribution (seconds/minutes) | Even distribution or regular spikes suggest bot activity | Human trading: irregular patterns |
| **Buy/Sell Ratio** | `buysellratio`, `buysellratioabs` | Proportion of buy vs sell market orders | Sustained deviation from 0.4-0.6 range or unnatural steadiness during volatility | Normal: 0.4-0.6 |
| **VWAP** | `vwap` | Volume-weighted average price | Significant deviation between VWAP and actual trading price | Context-dependent; deviations outside normal fluctuation are suspicious |

---

### Critical Correlation Patterns

These simultaneous anomalies strongly indicate market manipulation:

**Pattern 1: Artificial Volume**
- Volume distribution deviates from power law (unusual whale trades)
- AND Volume-volatility correlation < 0.4
- *Interpretation:* Large trades not causing expected volatility suggests wash trading

**Pattern 2: Data Manipulation**
- First-digit distribution significantly deviates from Benford's Law
- AND K-S test value > critical value
- *Interpretation:* Strong evidence of fabricated trading data

**Pattern 3: Price Manipulation**
- VWAP significantly differs from market price
- AND Buy/sell ratio outside 0.4-0.6 range
- *Interpretation:* Strategic buying/selling to influence perceived price

**Pattern 4: Bot Activity**
- Time-of-trade shows regular patterns or even distribution
- AND Buy/sell ratio anomalies
- *Interpretation:* Automated systems creating false market sentiment

---

### Output Rules

**Include ONLY:**
- Information explicitly present in the provided data
- Specific timestamps when anomalies occurred
- Exact metric values and thresholds exceeded
- Quantitative evidence supporting claims

**Do NOT:**
- Speculate beyond the data provided
- Invent specific values or timestamps not in the data
- Over-interpret minor deviations
- Create alarmist reports without substantial evidence

**If No Anomalies:**
Concisely state: "No significant anomalous patterns detected." Include brief evidence summary (e.g., "All metrics within normal ranges: vvcorrelation 0.67, K-S test below critical value, buy/sell ratio 0.48.").
