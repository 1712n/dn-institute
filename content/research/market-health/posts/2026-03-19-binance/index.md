## Summary

1. **$4.3 Billion Settlement:** In November 2023, Binance agreed to the largest corporate penalty in US financial enforcement history — $4.3 billion across DOJ, CFTC, FinCEN, and OFAC — for violations of the Bank Secrecy Act, sanctions regulations, and money transmission laws.
2. **CEO Criminal Guilty Plea:** Changpeng Zhao (CZ), Binance's founder and CEO, pleaded guilty to violating the Bank Secrecy Act and was sentenced to four months in federal prison — the first CEO of a major crypto exchange to face criminal prosecution.
3. **Wash Trading via Sigma Chain:** The SEC's June 2023 complaint alleged that Sigma Chain, a trading entity owned and controlled by CZ, engaged in wash trading on Binance.US during its first months of operation, artificially inflating trading volume to attract customers.
4. **Commingling of Customer Funds:** The SEC alleged that billions of dollars in customer funds were commingled with corporate funds and diverted to Merit Peak, another CZ-controlled entity.
5. **Compliance Failures:** Internal communications revealed that Binance deliberately avoided implementing anti-money laundering controls, with the former chief compliance officer writing in a 2018 chat that "we are operating as a fking unlicensed securities exchange in the USA bro."

## Background

Binance, founded in 2017 by Changpeng Zhao (CZ), grew to become the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange, processing over $32 billion in daily trading volume at its peak and commanding approximately 60% of global crypto spot trading market share by late 2022. The exchange operated without a fixed headquarters and initially resisted regulatory registration in major markets. Binance.US was established in 2019 as a nominally independent US-facing entity, operated by BAM Trading Services under license from Binance Holdings.

## Metrics used

### Sigma Chain wash trading — Artificial volume inflation

The SEC's complaint (filed June 5, 2023, *SEC v. Binance Holdings Ltd.*, Case No. 1:23-cv-01599, D.D.C.) detailed how wash trading was used to inflate volumes on the Binance.US platform:

- **Sigma Chain AG**, a Swiss-registered trading firm, was **owned and controlled by CZ** and served as one of the primary market makers on Binance.US.
- During the **first months of Binance.US operations in 2019**, Sigma Chain engaged in wash trading — simultaneously acting as buyer and seller — to create the appearance of liquid markets and attract retail customers.
- The SEC alleged that Sigma Chain's wash trades artificially inflated trading volumes by matched self-trading, making the platform appear more active than it actually was.
- CZ maintained personal control over Sigma Chain while simultaneously controlling the exchange itself — a structural conflict of interest with no regulatory disclosure.

### Commingling and diversion of customer funds

The SEC complaint also alleged:

- **Merit Peak Limited**, another CZ-controlled entity, received **billions of dollars** in transfers from Binance.US customer accounts held at Silvergate Bank.
- Customer funds were **commingled** with Binance corporate funds in accounts held at Silvergate and Signature banks.
- These transfers occurred without customer knowledge or consent.
- BAM Trading (Binance.US operator) customers were "deceived regarding the controls in place to safeguard customer assets."

### Deliberate anti-compliance culture

Internal communications cited in the DOJ and CFTC proceedings revealed a deliberate strategy to avoid regulatory obligations:

- **2018 (Chief Compliance Officer):** "We are operating as a fking unlicensed securities exchange in the USA bro."
- **2019 (Chief Compliance Officer to colleague):** When asked about suspicious transactions, replied that the goal was not to flag them but to "get them to a point where they wont bother us."
- **2020 (Senior Executive):** Internal discussions about creating a US subsidiary (Binance.US) specifically to act as a "regulatory shield" while maintaining actual operational control from the global entity.
- **Sanctions evasion:** Binance processed transactions for users in sanctioned jurisdictions including Iran, Syria, Cuba, and Russian-occupied regions of Ukraine. Internal records showed the exchange facilitated over **$898 million** in transactions between US users and users in sanctioned jurisdictions.

## Regulatory actions and legal outcomes

### Department of Justice — November 21, 2023

CZ pleaded guilty to one count of violating the Bank Secrecy Act by failing to maintain an effective anti-money laundering (AML) program. He agreed to:

- **$50 million personal penalty**
- Step down as Binance CEO (replaced by Richard Teng)
- **Four months in federal prison** (sentenced April 30, 2024 by Judge Richard Jones, Western District of Washington)
- Three years of supervised release
- Not serve in any Binance leadership role for three years

Binance Holdings pleaded guilty to violations of the Bank Secrecy Act, sanctions laws, and money transmission regulations, agreeing to:

- **$4.3 billion in total penalties** (the combined amount includes payments to multiple agencies)
- Retain an independent compliance monitor for five years
- Implement comprehensive AML and sanctions compliance programs
- Maintain complete records available to US authorities

Attorney General Merrick Garland described it as "the largest corporate resolution to include a criminal guilty plea."

### CFTC — March 2023 (complaint) / November 2023 (settlement)

On **March 27, 2023**, the CFTC filed a complaint against Binance and CZ (*CFTC v. Binance Holdings Ltd.*, Case No. 1:23-cv-01887, N.D. Ill.), alleging:

- Offering unregistered crypto derivatives to US customers
- Failing to register as a futures commission merchant (FCM)
- CZ directing employees to help US customers circumvent compliance controls using VPNs and shell companies

As part of the November 2023 global settlement, Binance agreed to pay **$2.7 billion** to the CFTC — the largest civil monetary penalty in CFTC history.

### FinCEN — November 21, 2023

The Financial Crimes Enforcement Network assessed a **$3.4 billion civil money penalty** against Binance for willful violations of the Bank Secrecy Act, including operating an unlicensed money transmitting business and failing to file suspicious activity reports (SARs). This is the largest enforcement action in FinCEN history.

### OFAC — November 21, 2023

The Office of Foreign Assets Control reached a **$968 million settlement** with Binance for processing transactions that violated US sanctions programs.

### SEC — June 5, 2023 (ongoing)

The SEC filed 13 charges against Binance, CZ, and BAM Trading, including:

- Operating unregistered exchanges, broker-dealers, and clearing agencies
- Offering and selling unregistered securities (including BNB, BUSD, and staking programs)
- Wash trading via Sigma Chain
- Commingling customer funds

As of early 2026, the SEC case remains ongoing.
