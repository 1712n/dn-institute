## Summary

1. **Negative volume-distribution skewness is the standout anomaly.** On 1 May 2024, the log of executed quote-volume on the FLOKI/USDT spot market had a skewness of **-0.60**, while BTC/USDT (+0.25), ETH/USDT (+0.18) and PEPE/USDT (+0.01) were all positive or near zero. Healthy retail-driven markets are right-skewed (many small trades, few large ones); FLOKI is the only market here that inverts this, a pattern consistent with synthetic, evenly sized order flow.
2. **A single whole-token lot dominates FLOKI's order flow.** The exact size **1,500,000 FLOKI** (about $231 at the prevailing price) was executed **6,197 times** in one day, and round mega-lots of 1,000,000 and 500,000 FLOKI rank in the top five most-frequent sizes. Repeated identical block sizes are a recognised fingerprint of volume-generating bots.
3. **The contrast with organic markets is structural, not cosmetic.** BTC and ETH also concentrate trades at a single size, but that size is the exchange dust minimum (0.0001 BTC ~ $5.77; 0.0019 ETH ~ $5.55), which is genuine retail micro-activity. FLOKI's dominant lot is a deliberately chosen large round number, not a minimum-tick artefact.
4. **The volume tail looks superficially normal, which is the point.** FLOKI's Hill tail exponent (2.79) sits inside the conventional sub-3 band, so a naive tail test passes. The manipulation is visible only when the *shape* (skew) and the *exact-size repetition* are examined together.
5. **Methodology is fully reproducible.** All conclusions are derived from Binance's public executed-trade archive. The exact files, row counts and SHA-256 hashes are published in this folder, together with the analysis script, so every figure can be regenerated independently.

## Data and methodology

This study uses **executed aggregate trades** (`aggTrades`), not order-book snapshots or third-party summaries. Each record is a real fill: price, quantity, timestamp and the maker/taker flag. The data comes from Binance's public market-data archive:

```
https://data.binance.vision/data/spot/daily/aggTrades/<SYMBOL>/<SYMBOL>-aggTrades-2024-05-01.zip
```

We analysed the full UTC trading day of 1 May 2024 for four markets:

Market | Executed trades (day) | Source ZIP size
------ | ---------------------: | --------------:
FLOKI/USDT | 444,956 | 7,165,792 bytes
BTC/USDT | 1,852,572 | 25,789,838 bytes
ETH/USDT | 1,034,268 | (control)
PEPE/USDT | 206,310 | (control)

The SHA-256 checksums of the FLOKI and BTC archives are recorded in `DATASET-MANIFEST.txt` in this directory, and the full analysis pipeline is in `analysis.py`. BTC, ETH and PEPE serve as a control group: BTC and ETH are the deepest, most organic spot markets on the venue, and PEPE is a second meme token included so that meme-coin behaviour is not confused with manipulation behaviour.

The metrics below follow the [market-health metric definitions](https://dn.institute/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/) used elsewhere in this wiki.

## Metric 1 — Volume distribution skewness

In a market driven by independent participants, executed-trade volume follows a heavy-tailed, **right-skewed** distribution: a large number of small retail fills and a small number of large institutional fills. After a log transform, that asymmetry should leave a **positive** skewness. Synthetic order flow generated by a wash-trading or order-printing bot, by contrast, tends to cluster around a narrow band of pre-programmed sizes, flattening or even inverting the skew.

{{< figure src="volume-distribution.png" alt="FLOKI vs BTC executed quote-volume distribution" caption="Executed quote-volume distribution (log10 USDT) for FLOKI/USDT and BTC/USDT, Binance spot, 1 May 2024. FLOKI shows a compressed, left-leaning body; BTC shows the conventional right-skewed retail shape." >}}

The measured log-volume skewness across the four markets is:

Market | Log-volume skewness | Interpretation
------ | ------------------: | --------------
BTC/USDT | +0.25 | Normal right-skew (organic)
ETH/USDT | +0.18 | Normal right-skew (organic)
PEPE/USDT | +0.01 | Borderline / near symmetric
**FLOKI/USDT** | **-0.60** | **Negative skew — anomalous**

FLOKI is the only market in the set whose distribution is **negatively skewed**. A negative value means the bulk of the volume mass has been pushed *above* the typical small-trade region: there are proportionally too many medium-to-large fills of similar size and too few genuine small retail trades. This is the signature the Huobi study described as "below-zero skewness values that indicate volume manipulation practices", observed here on a single, isolated market rather than across a whole exchange.

## Metric 2 — Repeated exact-lot concentration (order printing)

Wash trading and volume-inflation bots typically replay a fixed order template. The clearest way to detect this is to count how often the *exact same quantity* is executed.

{{< figure src="floki-repeated-lots.png" alt="FLOKI most repeated exact trade sizes" caption="The twelve most frequently executed exact trade sizes on FLOKI/USDT, Binance, 1 May 2024. Whole-token mega-lots such as 1,500,000, 1,000,000 and 500,000 FLOKI dominate the leaderboard." >}}

The six most-repeated FLOKI lot sizes for the day were:

Exact lot size (FLOKI) | Times executed | Share of day's trades
---------------------: | -------------: | --------------------:
1,500,000 | 6,197 | 1.39%
261,746 | 1,719 | 0.39%
1,000,000 | 1,641 | 0.37%
125,326 | 1,549 | 0.35%
500,000 | 1,388 | 0.31%
375,051 | 808 | 0.18%

Three of the top five are perfectly round whole-token blocks (1.5M, 1M, 500k). Human discretionary traders do not place the *identical* 1,500,000-token order six thousand times a day; an automated order printer does. The single most-frequent lot value translated to roughly **$231** at the prevailing price of ~0.0001541 USDT/FLOKI, large enough to move reported volume yet small enough to attract little individual attention.

### Why the same metric is innocent on BTC and ETH

A purely mechanical "concentration" reading would actually flag BTC and ETH *harder* than FLOKI, and that is exactly why concentration must be read together with absolute trade value rather than in isolation:

Market | Most-frequent lot | Share of trades | USD value of that lot | Verdict
------ | ----------------- | --------------: | --------------------: | -------
BTC/USDT | 0.0001 BTC | 10.13% | ~$5.77 | Organic dust minimum
ETH/USDT | 0.0019 ETH | 16.14% | ~$5.55 | Organic dust minimum
FLOKI/USDT | 1,500,000 FLOKI | 1.39% | ~$231 | Deliberate round block

On BTC and ETH the dominant lot is the **exchange minimum** — thousands of genuine retail users buying ~$5 of coin. That is healthy micro-activity, not manipulation. On FLOKI the dominant lot is a **large round number chosen by a bot**, far above any dust floor. The same statistic therefore carries opposite meaning, and only the combination of *(a)* large absolute size and *(b)* high repetition isolates the manipulative pattern.

Concentration alone is not diagnostic — it must be weighted by the absolute USD value of the dominant lot." >}}

## Metric 3 — Tail exponent looks normal, by design

The volume-distribution tail-fitting indicator estimates how heavy the upper tail is. Traditional financial markets are expected to show a tail exponent below 3.

Market | Hill tail exponent (top 5%)
------ | -------------------------:
FLOKI/USDT | 2.79
BTC/USDT | 2.44

Taken alone, FLOKI's exponent of **2.79** sits comfortably inside the conventional sub-3 band and would *pass* a tail-only screen. This is an important caution for analysts: a manipulated market can deliberately keep its tail in the "normal" range while distorting the body of the distribution. Relying on a single indicator produces false negatives. The manipulation here is only exposed when the negative skew and the repeated mega-lot signature are layered on top of an otherwise unremarkable tail.

## Metric 4 — Intraday regularity

Genuine retail demand ebbs and flows with regional waking hours, news and price action, so both trade count and average lot size are volatile through the day. A volume program that runs on a fixed schedule produces a flatter, more mechanical profile.

{{< figure src="floki-intraday.png" alt="FLOKI intraday trade count and average lot size" caption="Hourly executed-trade count (bars) and average lot size (line) on FLOKI/USDT, Binance, 1 May 2024. The average lot size stays within a comparatively narrow band across the day rather than tracking organic intraday demand swings." >}}

The average FLOKI lot size holds within a relatively tight band across the 24-hour window, consistent with a persistent algorithmic participant rather than purely organic, news-driven retail flow.

## Benford cross-check

We also examined the leading-digit distribution of executed trade sizes against [Benford's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benford%27s_law), the technique this wiki has previously applied to Gate.io.

{{< figure src="benford-floki-vs-btc.png" alt="Leading-digit distribution vs Benford expectation" caption="Leading-digit distribution of executed trade sizes versus Benford's Law expectation, FLOKI/USDT and BTC/USDT, Binance, 1 May 2024." >}}

Benford's Law is *not* a clean discriminator on these particular markets, and we report that honestly: both pairs deviate from the expected distribution because their quantities are dominated by exchange tick/lot conventions (BTC by ~0.0001 dust sizes, FLOKI by whole-token rounding) rather than by free-running decimals. Benford analysis is therefore included as context, not as the load-bearing evidence. The skewness inversion and the repeated mega-lot signature, which are robust to tick-size effects, carry the conclusion.

## Conclusion

On 1 May 2024 the FLOKI/USDT spot market on Binance exhibited a combination of microstructure features that, taken together, are difficult to reconcile with organic trading:

- a **negative log-volume skewness (-0.60)** that none of the three control markets (BTC, ETH, PEPE) share;
- a **large round whole-token lot (1,500,000 FLOKI ~ $231) executed 6,197 times** in a single day, with further round mega-lots in the top five;
- an intraday lot-size profile that is flatter than organic retail flow would produce.

Crucially, the manipulation is **invisible to any single naive metric**: the tail exponent passes, the raw concentration figure is lower than BTC's, and Benford is muddied by tick conventions. It surfaces only through original, combined statistical analysis of the executed-trade record. Users and venues monitoring FLOKI/USDT should treat its reported volume with caution and weight repeated-lot concentration by absolute trade value rather than by frequency alone.
