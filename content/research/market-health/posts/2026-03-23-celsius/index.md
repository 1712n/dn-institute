## Summary

1. **$4.7 Billion Customer Losses:** Celsius Network, once one of the largest crypto lending platforms with over $20 billion in assets under management, filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in July 2022, leaving approximately 1.7 million customers unable to access their deposits.
2. **DOJ Fraud Charges:** In July 2023, the DOJ charged CEO Alex Mashinsky with seven counts of fraud and market manipulation, alleging he made false and misleading statements about Celsius's financial health while secretly selling his own CEL tokens.
3. **CEL Token Manipulation:** Mashinsky personally directed the purchase of hundreds of millions of dollars worth of CEL tokens to artificially inflate the price, while simultaneously selling his own holdings at the inflated prices — generating over $48 million in personal proceeds.
4. **Guilty Plea:** In December 2024, Mashinsky pleaded guilty to two counts of fraud, acknowledging that he deceived customers about the safety of their deposits and manipulated CEL token markets.
5. **FTC Settlement:** The FTC reached a $4.7 billion settlement with Celsius — the largest FTC action in the crypto space — permanently banning the company from handling consumer assets.

## Background

Celsius Network, founded in 2017 by Alex Mashinsky and Daniel Leon, operated as a crypto lending and borrowing platform. Celsius attracted retail depositors with promises of high-yield returns (up to 17% APY) on cryptocurrency deposits, positioning itself as a safer alternative to traditional banks. By early 2022, Celsius claimed over $20 billion in assets under management from 1.7 million customers. The platform's business model relied on lending deposited crypto to institutional borrowers and DeFi protocols at higher rates than it paid depositors.

## Metrics used

### False statements about financial health

According to the DOJ complaint (*United States v. Mashinsky*, S.D.N.Y., July 2023), Mashinsky made repeated public statements that were materially false:

- Claimed Celsius "never lost money" on customer deposits — while the company had in fact sustained hundreds of millions in losses from failed DeFi investments and the Terra/Luna collapse.
- Stated that customer assets were "safe" and that Celsius had "more assets than liabilities" — while internal records showed a growing deficit between customer deposits and available assets.
- Assured customers that Celsius was "not a bank" and did not lend out all deposits — while in reality, Celsius deployed nearly all customer assets in high-risk lending and DeFi strategies.

### CEL token manipulation

The manipulation of Celsius's native CEL token was central to the fraud:

- Mashinsky directed Celsius to spend **hundreds of millions of dollars** of company funds to purchase CEL tokens on the open market to support the token's price.
- While these company purchases artificially inflated the price, Mashinsky personally sold his own CEL holdings — generating over **$48 million** in personal proceeds.
- Former Chief Revenue Officer Roni Cohen-Pavon was also charged for his role in the CEL manipulation scheme. He pleaded guilty in September 2023.
- At its peak, CEL traded at approximately $8.00. After the manipulation was exposed, the token collapsed to under $0.10.

### Risky deployment of customer assets

Celsius deployed customer deposits into strategies far riskier than disclosed:

- Significant exposure to the **Anchor Protocol** on Terra/Luna — resulting in massive losses during the May 2022 UST collapse.
- Losses from the **stETH de-peg** — Celsius held large stETH positions that became illiquid.
- Concentrated exposure to **Grayscale GBTC** at a discount to NAV.
- Failed DeFi strategies and bridge exploits resulting in unrecoverable losses.

## Regulatory actions and legal outcomes

### Bankruptcy — July 13, 2022

Celsius paused customer withdrawals on **June 12, 2022**, and filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy on **July 13, 2022** in the Southern District of New York. The filing revealed a $1.2 billion deficit between customer deposits and available assets.

### DOJ criminal charges — July 13, 2023

The DOJ charged Alex Mashinsky with seven counts:

- Securities fraud
- Commodities fraud
- Wire fraud
- Conspiracy to commit securities fraud, commodities fraud, and wire fraud
- Conspiracy to manipulate the market for CEL token

### SEC civil action — July 13, 2023

The SEC filed a parallel civil complaint charging Mashinsky and Celsius with offering unregistered securities through the Celsius "Earn Interest Program" and with making materially false statements.

### CFTC action — July 13, 2023

The CFTC charged Celsius and Mashinsky with fraud and misrepresentation in connection with a scheme involving digital asset commodities.

### FTC settlement — July 2023

The FTC reached a **$4.7 billion settlement** with Celsius — the largest-ever FTC crypto enforcement action. The order permanently banned Celsius from offering products or services involving consumer assets.

### Mashinsky guilty plea — December 2024

Mashinsky pleaded guilty to **two counts**: commodities fraud and a fraudulent scheme to manipulate the CEL token. Sentencing was scheduled for April 2025, with prosecutors seeking a multi-year prison term.

### Cohen-Pavon guilty plea — September 2023

Former CRO Roni Cohen-Pavon pleaded guilty to charges related to the CEL token manipulation scheme and cooperated with prosecutors.
