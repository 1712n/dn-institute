## Summary

1. **Only 0.9% genuine volume**: Research by The Tie determined that LBank's expected trading volume was only **0.9% of its reported volume** — meaning 99.1% was fabricated.
2. **Overtook Binance on CoinMarketCap**: In March 2019, LBank briefly surpassed Binance on CoinMarketCap's adjusted trade volume rankings — an anomaly immediately flagged by independent researchers as evidence of systematic volume fabrication.
3. **Not among verified exchanges**: Bitwise Asset Management's March 2019 SEC presentation identified only 10 exchanges with verifiable genuine volume. LBank was not among them, and was included in the **95% of reported Bitcoin volume** estimated to be fake.
4. **Hacken audit concerns**: A 2019 audit by Hacken raised concerns about "inconsistencies in liquidity and signs of potentially random trading activity," casting doubt on the authenticity of LBank's reported volumes.

## Background

LBank was established in 2015 as a centralized cryptocurrency exchange. Originally focused on the Chinese market, LBank has operated internationally with limited regulatory oversight. Despite relatively low brand recognition compared to major exchanges, LBank periodically appeared in top-10 exchange rankings by reported volume — a discrepancy that attracted scrutiny from independent researchers.

## Volume Fabrication Evidence

### The Tie Research (March 2019)

The Tie, a cryptocurrency data analytics firm, published research in March 2019 that provided the most specific quantification of LBank's volume fabrication [1]:

- LBank's expected trading volume was **0.9% of its reported volume**
- This means that for every $100 million in reported daily volume, only approximately **$900,000** represented genuine trading activity
- Similarly analyzed Bit-Z showed **0.79%** real volume — confirming that the CoinMarketCap ranking anomaly was not limited to a single exchange

The methodology compared reported trading volumes against expected volumes derived from web traffic, social media engagement, and other indicators of genuine user activity.

### CoinMarketCap Rankings Anomaly (March 2019)

On March 21, 2019, both LBank and Bit-Z simultaneously overtook Binance — then and now the world's largest exchange by genuine volume — on CoinMarketCap's adjusted trade volume rankings [2]:

- LBank reached the **#1 position** on CoinMarketCap before settling at #3
- Binance dropped to #2
- Bit-Z held the top spot at the time of reporting

This ranking anomaly was particularly telling because:
- No corresponding increase in media coverage, user registrations, or web traffic accompanied LBank's rise
- The ascent coincided with CoinMarketCap implementing new "adjusted volume" metrics — which LBank and Bit-Z apparently gamed
- The rankings quickly corrected as CoinMarketCap refined its methodology further

### Bitwise Asset Management SEC Presentation (March 2019)

Bitwise's analysis for the SEC identified only **10 exchanges** with genuine, verifiable Bitcoin trading volume: Binance, Bitfinex, bitFlyer, Bitstamp, Bittrex, Coinbase Pro, Gemini, itBit, Kraken, and Poloniex [3].

LBank was not among these exchanges. Bitwise estimated that **95% of all reported Bitcoin trading volume** was fake, with exchanges like LBank exemplifying the problem. The presentation detailed how exchanges inflated volumes to:

- Attract **listing fee revenue** from token projects
- Gain **perceived legitimacy** through high CoinMarketCap rankings
- Drive **retail user deposits** from traders who used rankings as a proxy for trustworthiness

### Hacken Audit (2019)

Security and blockchain analytics firm Hacken conducted a 2019 audit of LBank that raised red flags [4]:

- The audit identified **inconsistencies in liquidity** metrics
- Signs of **potentially random trading activity** were observed
- The audit "raised suspicions about the authenticity of LBank's reported trading volumes"

While Hacken's assessment stopped short of definitive conclusions, the finding that a professional audit could identify "random" (algorithmically generated) trading patterns underscores the detectable nature of wash trading on the platform.

### Alameda Research (2019)

Alameda Research's analysis of 48 exchanges identified a group of 14 exchanges where genuine volume "might well be zero." While LBank was not explicitly named in all published summaries of the report, the exchange fell within the category of exchanges not verified as having genuine volume by any of the major independent analyses conducted in the 2018–2019 period [5].

## Regulatory Status

LBank operates with limited regulatory oversight:

- Based in Hong Kong with operations spanning multiple jurisdictions
- Faced regulatory issues in **Japan**, where the Financial Services Agency accused it of operating without proper registration
- Not licensed or registered with major regulatory bodies such as the SEC, CFTC, or FCA

The absence of regulatory accountability creates an environment where volume manipulation carries minimal legal risk, and where external audits or proof-of-reserves requirements are not enforced.

## Market Manipulation Implications

LBank's case illustrates a recurring pattern in cryptocurrency market manipulation:

1. **Volume fabrication** creates artificially high CoinMarketCap rankings
2. **High rankings** attract listing fees from token projects seeking visibility
3. **Listing fee revenue** funds continued wash trading operations
4. **Perceived legitimacy** attracts retail deposits, creating real revenue
5. The cycle repeats, with fabricated metrics driving genuine economic activity

The 0.9% genuine volume figure is particularly significant because it suggests that nearly all of LBank's reported market activity existed solely as a mechanism for gaming aggregator rankings — a pure volume fabrication operation with negligible organic trading.

## Relevance to Market Health Metrics

LBank's volume patterns demonstrate several indicators documented in the DN Institute [Market Health Metrics](https://dn.institute/research/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/) framework:

- **Volume-traffic divergence**: Reported trading volume vastly exceeded what web traffic and user metrics could support
- **Rankings anomalies**: Sudden ascent in rankings without corresponding market events
- **Audit red flags**: Professional audits identified algorithmically generated trading patterns
- **Cross-validation failure**: No independent analysis verified LBank's volume as genuine
