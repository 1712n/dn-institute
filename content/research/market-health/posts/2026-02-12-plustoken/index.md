## Summary

1. **$4.2 billion seized — largest crypto Ponzi by recovered assets**: PlusToken, operating from April 2018 to June 2019, defrauded over 2.6 million members across 3,293 pyramid layers by promising 6–18% monthly returns from a fabricated "AI-Dog" arbitrage trading bot — Chinese authorities seized 194,775 BTC, 833,083 ETH, and billions of other tokens valued at over $4.2 billion.
2. **Fabricated AI arbitrage product**: PlusToken claimed its returns were generated by an AI-powered trading bot called "AI-Dog" that exploited price differences across cryptocurrency exchanges — no such technology existed, and all returns were paid from new investor deposits in a classic Ponzi structure.
3. **Measurable Bitcoin price impact**: Chainalysis research found a "statistically significant relationship" between PlusToken fund liquidations through Huobi OTC brokers and Bitcoin price declines — including a specific $34 million cashout in September 2019 that preceded BTC falling from $10,000 to $8,000.
4. **109 years combined sentences**: Chinese courts convicted 14 operators in November 2020, with sentences ranging from 2 to 11 years and fines up to 6 million yuan, while all seized cryptocurrency was forfeited to the national treasury.
5. **Controlled over 1% of Bitcoin supply**: At its peak, PlusToken held approximately 194,775 BTC — representing over 1% of the total Bitcoin supply at the time — making its liquidation operations a market-moving event detectable through on-chain analysis.

## Background

### Origins and Founder

**PlusToken** was established by **Chen Bo** in early 2018, with operations beginning between **April and May 2018**. It was primarily Chinese-based but operated internationally, marketed as an all-in-one cryptocurrency wallet, investment platform, and "profit-sharing community." The platform falsely claimed to be a South Korean-developed crypto wallet and exchange [1].

The scheme operated from **April 6, 2018 to June 27, 2019**, according to Chinese court documents.

### The "AI-Dog" Arbitrage Bot

PlusToken's central product was an alleged AI-powered arbitrage trading bot called **"AI-Dog"** [2]:

- Claimed to use artificial intelligence to exploit price differences across cryptocurrency exchanges
- **Minimum deposit**: $500 USD in cryptocurrency to activate the bot
- **Maximum**: $50,000 per account
- **Promised returns**: 6–18% per month (approximately 0.33% per day)
- Returns were paid in **PLUS tokens** (total supply: 500,000,000), which the scheme claimed could reach $350 in value

**The reality**: No legitimate arbitrage or AI trading ever occurred. Returns were paid entirely from deposits made by newer investors. The court confirmed "no real business was involved."

### Pyramid Structure

PlusToken employed a multi-tiered referral system [3]:

- Users assigned to **four tiers** based on investment size
- Top-tier members received titles such as **"Big Boy"** and **"Great God"**
- Rewards increased based on the number of new investors recruited
- The primary recruitment channel was China's **WeChat** messaging app
- Operators promoted PlusToken through social media campaigns, paid advertisements, physical events, and even grocery store advertisements

### Geographic Spread

- **Primary market**: China — recruitment occurred extensively through WeChat groups, offline meetups, and public workshops
- **Secondary market**: South Korea — PlusToken falsely presented itself as a Korean product for legitimacy
- **Other markets**: Japan, Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Vietnam, Malaysia, Singapore), Germany, Canada

## The Collapse and Arrests

### Exit Scam (Late June 2019)

In **late June 2019**, users began experiencing withdrawal failures [4]:

- PlusToken initially blamed **"higher miner fees"** for delays
- Also falsely claimed a **"hacking attempt"** was responsible
- One blockchain transaction carried the message **"sorry we have run"** — confirming the exit scam
- The operators vanished with over **$3 billion** in cryptocurrency

### Vanuatu Arrests (June 27, 2019)

**Six Chinese nationals** were arrested in Vanuatu: Chen Bo, Yuan Yuan, Ding Zanqing, Peng Yixuan, Wang Renmi, and Dong Jianhua [5]:

- Four held Vanuatu citizenships obtained by investment
- Chinese authorities asserted they had **forged criminal record check documents** when obtaining citizenship
- Vanuatu law enforcement, accompanied by Chinese law enforcement representatives, carried out the arrests
- All six were deported to China

### Mass Arrests (July 2020)

China's **Ministry of Public Security** announced that all **27 major criminal suspects** and **82 key members** (109 total) had been arrested across Cambodia, Vanuatu, Vietnam, and Malaysia [6].

## Chinese Court Convictions

### Proceedings and Sentences

- **Court**: Yancheng Intermediate People's Court, Jiangsu Province
- **Initial ruling**: September 22, 2020 (district court)
- **Final appellate judgment**: November 19, 2020 — all appeals denied
- **Charge**: Organizing and leading a pyramid scheme (defrauding investors of 14.8 billion yuan / $2.25 billion)
- **Defendants**: 14 individuals including Chen Bo [8]

**Sentences**: Prison terms ranged from **2 to 11 years**, with fines from 120,000 yuan (~$18,285) to 6 million yuan (~$914,000). Combined sentences for all 14 defendants totaled approximately **109 years**.

Key defendants:
- **Chen Bo** (founder): Among the highest sentences (up to 11 years)
- **Chen Tao** (money launderer): 4 years — managed transferring and laundering illegal gains
- **Ding Zanqing**: Organized promotional activities
- **Peng Yixuan** and **Gu Zhijiang**: Managed outreach via WeChat and offline events

## Bitcoin Price Impact: Chainalysis Analysis

Chainalysis published detailed research tracking PlusToken's market impact [9]:

### Scale of Laundering
- Approximately **45,000 BTC** and **800,000 ETH** were definitively moved for laundering
- The stolen Bitcoin underwent more than **24,000 transfers** using more than **71,000 different addresses**
- Approximately **25,000 BTC** was liquidated through OTC brokers
- At least **$185 million** worth of stolen Bitcoin was cashed out through **26 prominent OTC brokers on Huobi**

### Price Correlation
- Chainalysis found a **"statistically significant relationship"** between PlusToken transfers to Huobi OTC brokers and Bitcoin price volatility
- **Specific case**: A large cashout of approximately **$34 million** around **September 20, 2019** preceded Bitcoin's price dropping from **~$10,000 to ~$8,000** between September 24–26, 2019
- Bitcoin-to-Tether trading volume on Huobi spiked starting September 23, correlating with on-chain BTC transfers to OTC brokers days prior
- **"Spikes in on-chain flow to OTC brokers correlate with drops in Bitcoin's price"**

### Caveats
Chainalysis acknowledged they could not prove causation: "it's possible that price drops follow the cashouts by coincidence but are in fact caused by something else." A March 2020 update noted minimal subsequent impact, with only 23,000 BTC sent to exchanges since December 2019.

## Money Laundering Methods

PlusToken operators employed multiple laundering techniques [10]:

1. **CoinJoin mixing**: Used Wasabi Wallet's CoinJoin technology to jumble similar amounts of Bitcoin — though their mixing was "sloppy" according to Chainalysis, as they reused addresses and likely ran multiple Wasabi clients simultaneously (a Sybil attack on the mixing protocol)
2. **Peel chains**: Funds sent through several wallets in quick succession, with small amounts broken off at each step
3. **OTC brokers on Huobi**: Nearly all funds ultimately liquidated through OTC brokers with lower KYC requirements, converting to Tether (USDT) as a stable intermediary
4. **Transaction obfuscation**: 45,000 BTC moved through 24,000+ transfers across 71,000+ unique addresses

## WoToken: The PlusToken Successor

After PlusToken's collapse, a nearly identical scheme called **WoToken** emerged, defrauding over **715,000 investors** of more than **$1 billion** before being shut down at the end of 2019. One of WoToken's core criminals was a former key member of PlusToken, demonstrating a direct operational link between the schemes [11].

## Market Manipulation Implications

The PlusToken scheme reveals critical vulnerabilities in cryptocurrency market integrity:

1. **Ponzi liquidation as market manipulation**: PlusToken's systematic liquidation of 194,775 BTC through OTC brokers created measurable downward pressure on Bitcoin's price — demonstrating that large-scale Ponzi cash-outs function as a form of market manipulation even without intent to manipulate markets
2. **OTC broker opacity as laundering enabler**: The concentration of PlusToken liquidations through 26 OTC brokers on Huobi — exploiting lower KYC requirements — demonstrates that the OTC tier of cryptocurrency markets remains a critical vulnerability for both laundering and undetected selling pressure
3. **Scale detection through on-chain analysis**: Chainalysis's ability to correlate PlusToken fund flows with price movements demonstrates that on-chain monitoring of known fraud addresses can provide real-time market health signals
4. **Cross-border enforcement gap**: Despite the scheme's scale ($4.2B), enforcement was limited to Chinese courts — international victims received no direct restitution, and the seized assets were forfeited to the Chinese national treasury rather than distributed to global victims

## Relevance to Market Health Metrics

PlusToken's case demonstrates several indicators in the DN Institute [Market Health Metrics](https://dn.institute/research/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/) framework:

- **Large-holder liquidation as market health signal**: PlusToken's systematic OTC sales of 194,775 BTC demonstrate that on-chain monitoring of large wallet movements — particularly those associated with known fraud — provides a measurable leading indicator of selling pressure and potential price impact
- **OTC flow monitoring**: The concentration of laundering through Huobi OTC brokers demonstrates that OTC transaction volumes, particularly those involving known tainted addresses, should be incorporated into exchange-level health assessments
- **Yield sustainability as universal fraud indicator**: Promised returns of 6–18% monthly from a crypto arbitrage bot represent unsustainable economics applicable across both CeFi platforms (PlusToken, Bitconnect, Celsius) and DeFi protocols (Anchor/UST), reinforcing that yield anomalies are the most reliable cross-platform fraud signal
- **Successor scheme detection**: The direct operational link between PlusToken and WoToken demonstrates that fraud operators recycle tactics — monitoring for new schemes with structural similarities to collapsed predecessors provides an early warning mechanism
