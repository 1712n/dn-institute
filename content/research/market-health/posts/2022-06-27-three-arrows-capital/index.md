## Summary 🌰

Three Arrows Capital (3AC), once one of the largest and most influential cryptocurrency hedge funds managing approximately $10 billion in peak assets, collapsed in June 2022 after years of overleveraged trading, misrepresentation to creditors, and market manipulation through concentrated positions in illiquid assets. Co-founders Su Zhu and Kyle Davies fled creditors, defied court orders, and were eventually subject to criminal prosecution. Su Zhu was arrested in September 2023 while attempting to flee Singapore. The fund's collapse triggered a contagion chain that brought down multiple crypto lenders and contributed to billions in losses across the industry. 🌰

## Background 🌰

### Origins and Growth

Three Arrows Capital was founded in 2012 by Su Zhu and Kyle Davies, former classmates at Phillips Academy and Columbia University. Originally operating as a traditional FX and emerging markets fund in Singapore, 3AC pivoted to cryptocurrency trading around 2017 and grew rapidly during the 2020-2021 crypto bull market. 🌰

By early 2022, 3AC had positioned itself as the "smart money" of crypto — a sophisticated institutional player whose endorsement of projects and tokens carried significant market weight. The fund's assets under management peaked at approximately $10 billion, and its public advocacy for certain tokens and protocols materially influenced market sentiment and pricing. 🌰

### Investment Strategy and Leverage

3AC's core strategy involved taking large, concentrated, highly leveraged positions in crypto assets and ecosystem tokens. Key positions included: 🌰

- **Grayscale Bitcoin Trust (GBTC)**: 3AC accumulated the single largest known GBTC position, exploiting the trust's premium-to-NAV structure. When the GBTC premium converted to a persistent discount in early 2021, the fund was trapped in a position that could not be unwound without massive losses.
- **LUNA/UST**: 3AC held a substantial position in the Terra ecosystem, reportedly investing approximately $200 million in LUNA and participating in Luna Foundation Guard financing.
- **Ethereum staked derivatives**: Significant positions in stETH (Lido staked ETH), which traded at a discount to ETH during the liquidity crisis, creating additional losses.
- **Solana, Avalanche, and Polkadot**: Large ecosystem token positions acquired through OTC deals and locked vesting schedules. 🌰

The fund financed these positions primarily through unsecured or undercollateralized borrowing from crypto lending platforms and trading desks, including Genesis Trading, Voyager Digital, BlockFi, Blockchain.com, and Deribit. 🌰

## Market Manipulation and Fraud 🌰

### GBTC Arbitrage Manipulation

3AC's most significant market manipulation involved the Grayscale Bitcoin Trust: 🌰

1. **Premium exploitation**: From 2020 to early 2021, GBTC traded at a premium to its underlying Bitcoin NAV (sometimes exceeding 40%). 3AC borrowed Bitcoin from lenders, deposited it into Grayscale to receive GBTC shares (subject to a 6-month lockup), and planned to sell the shares at the premium price after lockup expiration.

2. **Scale of positions**: 3AC became GBTC's largest known holder, accumulating over $2 billion in GBTC shares. The fund's concentrated buying distorted the GBTC premium itself, creating a reflexive loop where their accumulation signaled institutional demand, which attracted more capital, which inflated the premium further.

3. **Premium collapse**: In February 2021, the GBTC premium flipped to a discount as competing Bitcoin ETF products launched in Canada and expectations grew for a US spot Bitcoin ETF. The discount deepened throughout 2021 and 2022, reaching -30% or more. 3AC's massive GBTC position became deeply underwater, but the fund continued borrowing against these impaired assets without disclosing the true extent of losses to creditors. 🌰

### Misrepresentation to Creditors 🌰

As 3AC's positions deteriorated throughout late 2021 and into 2022, Zhu and Davies engaged in systematic fraud against their creditors:

- **Concealed losses**: The fund provided misleading or outdated NAV statements to lenders, overstating the fund's assets and understating its liabilities. Multiple creditors testified that 3AC's reported positions did not match their actual holdings.
- **Cross-collateralization fraud**: 3AC pledged the same collateral to multiple lenders simultaneously, borrowing far more in aggregate than its actual assets could support.
- **Undisclosed LUNA exposure**: The fund's $200 million LUNA position was not fully disclosed to all creditors prior to Terra's collapse in May 2022, which instantly vaporized a significant portion of the fund's remaining equity. 🌰

### Market Influence and Sentiment Manipulation

Beyond direct financial fraud, 3AC engaged in broader market manipulation through its public communications: 🌰

- **Token endorsements**: Su Zhu's public advocacy for specific tokens and ecosystems on social media (particularly Twitter) materially influenced retail and institutional buying decisions. When 3AC publicly supported a project, it often held significant pre-existing positions that benefited from the resulting price appreciation.
- **"Supercycle" narrative**: Zhu promoted the "crypto supercycle" thesis — the idea that crypto would see continuous appreciation without significant drawdowns — to justify extreme leverage and encourage others to maintain or increase their exposure. This narrative served 3AC's interest in maintaining the market conditions necessary for its leveraged positions to remain solvent.
- **Selective disclosure**: 3AC selectively disclosed its involvement in high-profile token launches and ecosystem investments to signal confidence, while concealing the deterioration of its core portfolio. 🌰

## The Collapse: June 2022 🌰

### Timeline

- **May 7-13**: Terra/LUNA collapse wipes out 3AC's ~$200M LUNA position
- **May-June**: Crypto market drawdown deepens 3AC's losses on GBTC, stETH, and altcoin positions. Margin calls from lenders accelerate.
- **June 14-15**: 3AC fails to meet margin calls from multiple lenders. Voyager Digital issues a notice of default on a $650 million loan. Genesis Trading reports losses of approximately $1.2 billion on 3AC exposure. 🌰
- **June 17**: 3AC's positions are forcibly liquidated on centralized exchanges including BitMEX, Deribit, and FTX.
- **June 27**: 3AC files for Chapter 15 bankruptcy in the Southern District of New York.
- **June 29**: Court-appointed liquidators from Teneo (BVI) begin asset recovery proceedings. Zhu and Davies have already fled Singapore. 🌰

### Creditor Losses

3AC's collapse resulted in approximately $3.5 billion in creditor claims: 🌰

| Creditor | Approximate Loss |
|----------|-----------------|
| Genesis Trading | $1.2 billion |
| Voyager Digital | $650 million |
| BlockFi | $80 million |
| Blockchain.com | $270 million |
| Deribit | $80 million |
| Others | ~$1.2 billion |

Voyager Digital filed for bankruptcy in July 2022 directly as a result of 3AC's default. Genesis Trading's losses contributed to the eventual collapse of its parent company Digital Currency Group's lending operations. BlockFi's losses from 3AC exposure weakened it sufficiently that it was subsequently swept up in the FTX collapse. 🌰

## Legal Proceedings 🌰

### Liquidation and Asset Recovery

Teneo, appointed as 3AC's liquidator, pursued global asset recovery: 🌰

- Identified that Zhu and Davies had transferred personal assets, including luxury properties in Singapore, to family members prior to the fund's collapse
- Traced fund assets to personal accounts, including art purchases and luxury goods
- Filed lawsuits against the co-founders personally for breach of fiduciary duty and fraud

### Criminal Prosecution 🌰

- **September 2023**: Su Zhu arrested at Singapore's Changi Airport while attempting to board a flight, reportedly trying to leave the jurisdiction. Sentenced to four months in prison for contempt of court and obstruction of the liquidation process.
- **Ongoing criminal investigations**: Authorities in Singapore, the US, and the BVI pursued criminal fraud charges against both co-founders for misrepresentation to creditors, misappropriation of fund assets, and obstruction of justice. 🌰

### OPNX Controversy

Following 3AC's collapse, Zhu and Davies launched Open Exchange (OPNX), a platform designed to trade bankruptcy claims. The venture drew widespread criticism as an attempt to profit from the very type of financial distress that 3AC had helped create. Singapore's MAS issued a public statement noting that OPNX was not licensed to operate in Singapore. The exchange ultimately ceased operations. 🌰

## Market Manipulation Indicators 🌰

### Concentration and Reflexivity

3AC's positions exemplify several manipulation patterns detectable through market surveillance:

1. **Single-entity market dominance**: 3AC's GBTC position was large enough to materially influence the trust's premium/discount dynamics. When a single fund holds a dominant position in an instrument, their buying and selling distorts price discovery for all other participants.

2. **Leverage opacity**: The fund's ability to borrow from multiple lenders without centralized reporting meant that no single creditor understood 3AC's true aggregate leverage. Cross-collateralization fraud was only discoverable post-collapse through forensic analysis. 🌰

3. **Social media influence correlated with positions**: Su Zhu's public token endorsements, when mapped against 3AC's known holdings, show a pattern of promoting assets in which the fund held significant positions — a form of market manipulation through informational advantage.

4. **Connected failure cascades**: 3AC was interconnected with virtually every major crypto lending platform. Its collapse demonstrated how concentrated counterparty risk in an opaque OTC market can transform a single fund's failure into a systemic crisis. 🌰

### Lessons for Surveillance

- **Cross-platform position monitoring**: No single lender could assess 3AC's risk because lending was bilateral and opaque. Centralized position reporting (similar to Form 13F for equities) would have revealed the fund's true leverage.
- **Social media correlation analysis**: Automated monitoring of influential accounts' token endorsements correlated with on-chain position data could identify potential manipulation.
- **Premium/discount reflexivity detection**: When a single entity's buying activity materially influences the premium of a closed-end fund or trust product, this represents a form of market manipulation that should trigger enhanced surveillance. 🌰
