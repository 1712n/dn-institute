## Summary

1. **Sigma Chain self-trading**: The SEC's June 2023 complaint revealed that Sigma Chain, a Swiss-incorporated trading firm **owned and controlled by Binance CEO CZ**, wash traded **48 out of 51 newly-listed tokens** on Binance.US between January–June 2022, with self-trading accounting for up to **99%** of reported volume on some pairs.
2. **DWF Labs market manipulation**: A Wall Street Journal investigation in May 2024 revealed that Binance's internal surveillance team found evidence of **$300 million in wash trades** by market maker DWF Labs during 2023. The head of Binance's surveillance team was fired one week after submitting the report.
3. **Zero-fee volume inflation**: Kaiko Research documented that Binance's zero-fee BTC trading program (July 2022 – March 2023) inflated the exchange's market share from **50% to 72%**. When zero-fee trading ended on March 22, 2023, BTC-USDT volume **dropped 90%** overnight.
4. **Regulatory outcomes**: Binance pleaded guilty to DOJ charges in November 2023, resulting in a **$4.3 billion** fine — one of the largest corporate penalties in U.S. history. CZ was sentenced to 4 months in prison.

## Sigma Chain: CZ's Personal Wash Trading Vehicle

The most direct evidence of wash trading on Binance comes from the SEC's 13-charge complaint filed in June 2023 [1]. The complaint details how Sigma Chain AG, a Swiss entity solely owned and controlled by Changpeng "CZ" Zhao, systematically wash traded on Binance.US (operated by BAM Trading):

- **48 out of 51 newly-listed tokens** were wash traded by Sigma Chain between January and June 2022
- In the three months preceding Binance.US's equity fundraise, Sigma Chain wash traded **51 of 58 crypto assets** available on the platform
- On certain tokens, Sigma Chain's self-trading constituted **up to 99%** of total reported volume
- Sigma Chain collected approximately **$190 million** for CZ during its operation
- Sigma Chain's expenditures included **$11 million on a yacht**

A critical structural concern was the overlap in personnel: Binance's back-office manager simultaneously served as Sigma Chain's President and held signatory authority over BAM Trading's bank accounts, creating a direct conduit for manipulated trading data to influence platform metrics.

The effect was to create an artificial appearance of liquidity and trading activity on Binance.US, which was then used to attract both retail users and institutional investment during BAM Trading's capital raise.

## DWF Labs: $300M in Wash Trades and Investigator Dismissal

In May 2024, the Wall Street Journal reported on an internal Binance investigation that uncovered systematic market manipulation by DWF Labs [2], one of the most active market makers in the crypto industry:

- DWF Labs conducted an estimated **$300 million in wash trades** on Binance during 2023
- DWF offered clients services to create "artificial volume" and drive up token prices
- The manipulation affected **Yield Guild Game (YGG)** and at least six other tokens
- After the head of Binance's surveillance team submitted findings, **he was fired within one week**

The dismissal of the investigator suggests institutional resistance to addressing wash trading, even when detected by the exchange's own compliance apparatus. DWF Labs denied the allegations, claiming its trading activity was legitimate market making [3].

## Zero-Fee Trading: Market Share Inflation

Kaiko Research provided quantitative evidence of how Binance's fee structure directly incentivized artificial volume [4]:

- In July 2022, Binance eliminated trading fees for BTC pairs
- Market share rose from approximately **50% to over 72%** of global centralized exchange volume
- Zero-fee trades accounted for **66% of Binance's total volume** at peak
- On **March 22, 2023**, when zero-fee trading ended: BTC-USDT daily volume **dropped 90%** in a single day
- Binance's market share immediately fell from **>70% to ~58%**

The extreme volume elasticity — a 90% decline from a fee change alone — strongly suggests that the majority of zero-fee era volume was not driven by genuine trading demand. In organic markets, fee changes produce gradual volume migrations; an overnight 90% collapse is characteristic of fee-sensitive wash trading ceasing when the cost barrier returns.

## Volume-Liquidity Divergence

Additional Kaiko analysis from October 2024 documented ongoing volume anomalies across the broader Binance ecosystem [5]:

- Several trading pairs exhibited **volume-to-liquidity ratios exceeding 100×**, where reported trading volume far exceeded the available order book depth
- Repetitive order patterns with fixed sizes were identified on multiple pairs
- Weekly trade patterns showed mechanical regularity inconsistent with organic human trading behavior

These metrics align with the DN Institute's [Market Health Metrics framework](https://dn.institute/research/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/), which flags volume-liquidity divergence as a primary indicator of artificial trading.

## Regulatory Actions and Legal Outcomes

The regulatory response to Binance's practices has been the most significant enforcement action in cryptocurrency history:

| Authority | Date | Charges | Outcome |
|-----------|------|---------|---------|
| U.S. DOJ | Nov 2023 | BSA violations, unlicensed MSB, IEEPA sanctions violations | **$4.3 billion fine** |
| U.S. CFTC | Nov 2023 | Unregistered derivatives exchange, evasion of compliance | **$2.7 billion** ($1.35B disgorgement + $1.35B penalty) |
| U.S. SEC | Jun 2023 | 13 charges including operating unregistered exchange and broker-dealer | Dismissed May 2025 |
| CZ Personal | Apr 2024 | Criminal BSA violations | **4 months imprisonment** + $200M in personal fines |

The DOJ determined that Binance facilitated approximately **$1 billion** in payments to sanctioned entities including Hamas and actors in North Korea. The exchange had never filed a single Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) with FinCEN prior to the enforcement action [6].

## The "Tai Chi" Strategy

Additional context comes from a leaked 2018 internal strategy document, first reported by Forbes in October 2020 [7]. The document described creating a U.S. entity (later Binance.US / BAM Trading) as a regulatory decoy:

- The U.S. entity was explicitly described as a "bait and switch" to distract regulators
- The document recommended "strategic" VPN usage to evade SEC, CFTC, and OFAC enforcement
- The ultimate plan: Binance would "acquire the US operation at a nominal price" once it had served its purpose

This document demonstrates that regulatory evasion was not incidental but central to Binance's corporate strategy, providing the operational cover under which wash trading programs like Sigma Chain could operate without detection.

## Conclusion

The evidence of wash trading on Binance is documented across multiple independent sources: federal court filings with specific trade counts and percentages (SEC), investigative journalism with named sources (WSJ), quantitative market data analysis (Kaiko), and the exchange's own internal investigation (DWF Labs report). The combination of a CEO-controlled wash trading entity, suppression of internal compliance findings, and fee structures that incentivize artificial volume represents a comprehensive pattern of market manipulation at the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange.
