## Summary 🌰

The collapse of the Terra blockchain ecosystem in May 2022 — including the TerraUSD (UST) algorithmic stablecoin and its companion token LUNA — represented one of the largest single market manipulation events and value destruction episodes in cryptocurrency history. Approximately $40 billion in market capitalization was wiped out over a span of five days. Terraform Labs co-founder Do Kwon was subsequently convicted of securities fraud and sentenced to federal prison, with evidence showing he manipulated UST's peg, deceived investors about the protocol's stability mechanisms, and misappropriated billions in proceeds. 🌰

## Background 🌰

### The Algorithmic Stablecoin Mechanism

Terra was a blockchain platform launched in 2018 by Terraform Labs, a South Korean company co-founded by Do Kwon and Daniel Shin. Its core product was TerraUSD (UST), an algorithmic stablecoin designed to maintain a 1:1 peg with the US dollar without holding dollar reserves. Instead, UST relied on an arbitrage mechanism tied to LUNA, Terra's native token: 🌰

- **Minting UST**: Users could burn $1 worth of LUNA to mint 1 UST
- **Redeeming UST**: Users could burn 1 UST to receive $1 worth of LUNA
- **Peg maintenance**: If UST traded below $1, arbitrageurs could buy cheap UST and redeem it for $1 of LUNA, pocketing the difference and reducing UST supply. If UST traded above $1, arbitrageurs could mint new UST by burning LUNA, increasing supply. 🌰

This mechanism was inherently reflexive: it depended on sustained confidence in LUNA's value to guarantee UST redemptions. If confidence in LUNA collapsed, the arbitrage mechanism would fail, and both tokens would enter a death spiral. 🌰

### Anchor Protocol: The Unsustainable Yield Engine

The primary driver of UST adoption was Anchor Protocol, a DeFi lending platform on the Terra blockchain that offered approximately 19.5% APY on UST deposits. At its peak, Anchor held approximately $14 billion in UST deposits — roughly 72% of all UST in circulation. 🌰

The 19.5% yield was not generated by market lending activity. Anchor's actual lending yields were typically in the 5-8% range. The difference was subsidized by Terraform Labs through a "yield reserve" that was periodically topped up with LUNA sales proceeds. This meant the entire system's growth depended on continuous LUNA price appreciation and new capital inflows — a structure characteristic of a Ponzi scheme. 🌰

By March 2022, Anchor's yield reserve had declined from $70 million to approximately $20 million, prompting Terraform Labs to inject another $450 million. This injection temporarily restored confidence but highlighted the fundamental unsustainability of the model. 🌰

## The Collapse: May 7-13, 2022 🌰

### Triggering Events

On May 7, 2022, large UST withdrawals from Anchor (approximately $2 billion) and coordinated selling on Curve Finance liquidity pools pushed UST below its $1 peg. The sequence of events: 🌰

1. **May 7**: UST depegs to $0.985. Approximately $2.3 billion withdrawn from Anchor Protocol. The Luna Foundation Guard (LFG), a reserve entity established by Terraform Labs, begins deploying its $3.5 billion Bitcoin reserve to defend the peg.

2. **May 8**: UST briefly recovers to $0.995 after LFG deploys Bitcoin reserves, but selling pressure resumes. 🌰

3. **May 9**: UST drops to $0.69. The arbitrage mechanism begins the death spiral: massive UST redemptions mint enormous quantities of LUNA, crashing LUNA's price, which reduces the backing for further UST redemptions, which triggers more UST selling. LUNA falls from approximately $60 to $30.

4. **May 10**: UST hits $0.30. LUNA drops below $5. Terraform Labs halts the Terra blockchain twice to prevent governance attacks as LUNA's market cap briefly falls below UST's. 🌰

5. **May 11-12**: UST trades between $0.15-$0.30. LUNA's supply hyperinflates from 345 million to over 6.5 trillion tokens as the mint/burn mechanism runs unchecked. LUNA's price approaches zero.

6. **May 13**: Terra blockchain halted permanently for the original chain. LUNA trades at approximately $0.0001. UST at approximately $0.10. Combined market cap destroyed: approximately $40 billion. 🌰

### The Bitcoin Reserve Deployment

The Luna Foundation Guard had accumulated a Bitcoin reserve of approximately 80,394 BTC (worth approximately $3.5 billion at the time) ostensibly to defend the UST peg in emergencies. When the depeg began, LFG deployed most of this reserve: 🌰

- Approximately 52,189 BTC was sent to market makers (including Genesis Trading and Jump Trading)
- The BTC was sold on open markets to purchase UST, creating significant selling pressure on Bitcoin itself (contributing to BTC's decline from $36,000 to $26,000 during this period)
- The deployment was ultimately insufficient to restore the peg, and the remaining reserves (~313 BTC) were the subject of transparency controversies 🌰

### Impact on Bitcoin Markets

The LFG's emergency sale of approximately $2.8 billion in Bitcoin during a five-day window contributed to broader cryptocurrency market contagion. Bitcoin's price declined approximately 28% during May 2022, with the concentrated selling from LFG representing a significant fraction of the sell-side pressure during the critical May 9-11 period. This made the Terra collapse not just an internal failure but a source of market-wide manipulation, as the forced BTC liquidation distorted price discovery across the entire cryptocurrency market. 🌰

## Market Manipulation Evidence 🌰

### Concealed Peg Interventions

Evidence presented at trial and in SEC filings revealed that UST's peg had been artificially maintained on multiple occasions prior to the final collapse: 🌰

1. **May 2021 depeg**: UST briefly lost its peg, dropping to approximately $0.96. Do Kwon secretly arranged for Jump Trading (a market maker) to purchase large quantities of UST to restore the peg. This intervention was not disclosed to UST holders or LUNA investors. Kwon subsequently claimed in public appearances that the algorithmic mechanism had "worked exactly as designed" — a materially false statement that deceived investors about UST's true stability characteristics.

2. **Concealed LFG arrangements**: The Luna Foundation Guard's relationship with market makers was structured to obscure Terraform Labs' role in price support operations. Investors in LUNA were led to believe UST's peg was algorithmically maintained when it was, in fact, repeatedly propped up by discretionary trading interventions. 🌰

### Misrepresentation of Financial Model

Do Kwon made numerous public claims about UST and LUNA that constituted securities fraud:

- **Decentralization claims**: Kwon claimed UST was "decentralized" and not controlled by any single entity, when in practice Terraform Labs exercised significant control over the protocol's parameters and repeatedly intervened in markets
- **Yield sustainability**: Kwon defended Anchor's 19.5% yield as sustainable and organic, when internal communications showed Terraform Labs knew the yield was subsidized and unsustainable
- **Reserve adequacy**: Kwon touted the LFG Bitcoin reserve as sufficient protection against depeg events, while internal analysis suggested the reserve was inadequate for a large-scale loss of confidence 🌰

### Misappropriation of Funds

Following the collapse, investigations revealed that Terraform Labs had channeled approximately $100 million in crypto assets to a Swiss bank account controlled by Do Kwon, even as the ecosystem was collapsing and depositors were losing their life savings. Additionally, Do Kwon and associates had withdrawn substantial sums from the Luna Foundation Guard reserves for personal use. 🌰

## Legal Proceedings 🌰

### SEC Civil Action

On February 16, 2023, the SEC filed a civil complaint against Terraform Labs and Do Kwon, alleging:

- Fraud in connection with the offer and sale of securities (LUNA, wLUNA, and MIR tokens)
- Fraud regarding UST's stability
- Failure to register crypto asset securities 🌰

In April 2024, a federal jury in Manhattan found Terraform Labs and Do Kwon liable for civil fraud. The SEC subsequently reached a $4.5 billion settlement with Terraform Labs (largely uncollectable given the company's bankruptcy) and secured a personal judgment against Kwon. 🌰

### Criminal Prosecution

Do Kwon fled South Korea following the collapse and was arrested in Montenegro in March 2023 while attempting to travel using a forged Costa Rican passport. After extradition proceedings, Kwon was eventually extradited to the United States in late 2024. 🌰

In January 2025, Do Kwon pleaded guilty to securities fraud and wire fraud charges in the Southern District of New York. He admitted to making materially false statements about the stability and reliability of UST, concealing the Jump Trading intervention during the May 2021 depeg, and misappropriating investor funds. Kwon was sentenced to multiple years in federal prison. 🌰

### Korean Authorities

Daniel Shin, Terraform Labs co-founder, faced separate prosecution in South Korea for his role in the early development of the Terra ecosystem. South Korean authorities also pursued charges against several other Terraform Labs executives and employees. 🌰

## Manipulation Indicators and Market Surveillance Lessons 🌰

### Death Spiral Dynamics

The Terra collapse exemplifies a class of manipulation risk unique to algorithmic stablecoins:

- **Reflexive token economics**: When the stabilization mechanism depends on the value of a companion token that itself derives value from the stablecoin's adoption, any loss of confidence creates a self-reinforcing death spiral
- **Concentrated yield dependencies**: When the majority of a stablecoin's demand comes from a single yield source (Anchor), withdrawal from that source can trigger a disproportionate supply shock 🌰
- **Reserve opacity**: The LFG's Bitcoin reserve created a false sense of security while its deployment mechanism was untested and ultimately insufficient

### Surveillance Red Flags 🌰

1. **Yields exceeding market rates**: Anchor's 19.5% APY on a USD-pegged asset, in a period when DeFi lending rates averaged 3-5%, should have been a primary surveillance indicator
2. **Concentration risk**: 72% of UST supply concentrated in a single protocol (Anchor) represented extreme fragility
3. **Hidden market maker interventions**: The concealed Jump Trading peg restoration in May 2021 was a direct form of market manipulation that obscured UST's true risk profile
4. **Bitcoin reserve correlation**: LFG's accumulation of 80,000+ BTC created a systemic risk where UST's failure would directly impact Bitcoin markets through forced liquidation
5. **Founder flight risk**: Do Kwon's departure from South Korea and use of forged documents demonstrated consciousness of guilt and the importance of monitoring key persons in high-risk projects 🌰

### Broader Market Impact

The Terra collapse was a proximate cause of the 2022 crypto credit crisis, triggering a chain of failures:

- **Three Arrows Capital**: Held significant LUNA exposure, collapsed in June 2022
- **Celsius Network**: Already stressed, the broader market downturn accelerated its insolvency
- **Voyager Digital**: Exposed through Three Arrows Capital, filed bankruptcy in July 2022
- **FTX/Alameda**: The credit contagion from these failures contributed to the conditions that exposed FTX's own fraud in November 2022 🌰

Total estimated losses across the contagion chain exceeded $100 billion in market capitalization, making the Terra/LUNA collapse arguably the most consequential single event in cryptocurrency market history. 🌰
