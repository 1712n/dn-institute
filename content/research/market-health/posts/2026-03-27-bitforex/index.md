## Summary

1. **$56.5 Million Drained:** In February 2024, BitForex — a Seychelles-registered cryptocurrency exchange — became inaccessible after approximately $56.5 million in cryptocurrency was withdrawn from its hot wallets in a series of suspicious transactions.
2. **Unannounced Shutdown:** The exchange went offline without notice, with its website, customer support, and social media channels becoming unresponsive. No bankruptcy filing, regulatory notice, or customer communication was issued.
3. **On-Chain Evidence:** Blockchain analytics firms identified large outflows from BitForex hot wallets across Ethereum, Bitcoin, and Tron networks, with funds moved to previously unused wallets in a pattern consistent with insider extraction rather than external hacking.
4. **Prior Volume Fraud:** BitForex had previously been flagged by multiple researchers and the Blockchain Transparency Institute for reporting inflated trading volumes, with studies estimating that over 90% of its reported volume was fabricated through wash trading.
5. **Regulatory Void:** Operating from the Seychelles with no meaningful regulatory oversight, BitForex had no obligation to maintain proof of reserves, file reports with financial regulators, or protect customer assets — highlighting the risks of unregulated offshore exchanges.

## Background

BitForex was founded in 2018 by Garrett Jin (also known as Jason Luo) and registered in the Republic of Seychelles. The exchange marketed itself as a global platform serving users in over 200 countries, offering spot trading, derivatives, and various DeFi products. Despite claiming significant trading volumes, BitForex had been repeatedly identified in industry reports as an exchange with predominantly fake volume.

In February 2024, users reported being unable to access the platform or withdraw funds. On-chain investigators subsequently identified a pattern of large withdrawals from BitForex-controlled hot wallets that drained the exchange's reserves.

## Metrics used

### On-chain drain pattern

The withdrawal pattern identified by blockchain analysts was consistent with an insider exit rather than an external attack:

- Multiple large transactions from known BitForex hot wallets to fresh, previously unused addresses.
- Transfers occurred across **Ethereum, Bitcoin, and Tron** networks simultaneously, suggesting coordinated multi-chain extraction.
- Total outflow estimated at approximately **$56.5 million** based on blockchain data analysis.
- No evidence of smart contract exploitation, private key compromise from external actors, or other indicators of a traditional hack.
- The draining occurred over a period of days before the exchange went fully offline.

### Historical volume inflation

BitForex had a well-documented history of volume fabrication:

- The **Blockchain Transparency Institute** (BTI) consistently ranked BitForex among exchanges with the highest proportion of fake volume.
- The **Bitwise Asset Management** report submitted to the SEC in 2019 identified BitForex as one of several exchanges where over **95% of reported volume was wash trading**.
- CoinMarketCap introduced adjusted volume metrics partially in response to volume inflation by exchanges including BitForex.

### Exit scam indicators

Several factors pointed to a planned exit rather than an operational failure:

- No communication was issued to users before, during, or after the shutdown.
- The exchange's social media accounts stopped posting without explanation.
- Customer support channels became completely unresponsive.
- No law enforcement report, bankruptcy filing, or regulatory notice was published.
- The exchange's CEO and key personnel became unreachable.
