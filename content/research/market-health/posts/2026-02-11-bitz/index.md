## Summary

1. **Only 0.79% genuine volume**: Research by The Tie determined that Bit-Z's expected trading volume was only **0.79% of its reported volume** — meaning over 99.2% was fabricated.
2. **Overtook Binance on CoinMarketCap**: In March 2019, Bit-Z simultaneously surpassed Binance alongside LBank on CoinMarketCap's adjusted trade volume rankings — a ranking anomaly immediately attributed to systematic volume fabrication by independent researchers.
3. **Not among verified exchanges**: Bitwise Asset Management's March 2019 SEC presentation identified only 10 exchanges with verifiable genuine volume. Bit-Z was not among them, falling within the **95% of reported Bitcoin volume** estimated to be fake.
4. **469x volume inflation (BTI)**: The Blockchain Transparency Institute's December 2018 report found Bit-Z inflating volume by 469 times, with peak reported daily volumes exceeding $1.5 billion against negligible genuine activity.
5. **Transaction mining incentives**: Bit-Z operated a "transaction mining" model that returned trading fees as exchange tokens (BZ), creating direct financial incentives for wash trading.

## Background

Bit-Z was founded in 2016 and headquartered in Hong Kong. The exchange marketed itself as a global digital asset trading platform and expanded to ten countries by 2018. At its peak, Bit-Z claimed to be one of the top-10 exchanges by reported volume. Following the March 2019 ranking controversy and China's ongoing cryptocurrency crackdown, Bit-Z announced its closure, joining the wave of China-linked exchanges shutting down operations.

## Volume Fabrication Evidence

### The Tie Research (March 2019)

The Tie, a cryptocurrency data analytics firm, published research in March 2019 that quantified Bit-Z's volume fabrication alongside other exchanges [1]:

- Bit-Z's expected trading volume was **0.79% of its reported volume**
- This was the lowest genuine-to-reported ratio among the exchanges analyzed, even lower than LBank (0.9%)
- For every $100 million in reported daily volume, only approximately **$790,000** represented genuine trading activity
- The methodology compared reported trading volumes against expected volumes derived from web traffic, social media engagement, and other indicators of genuine user activity

### CoinMarketCap Rankings Anomaly (March 2019)

On March 21, 2019, Bit-Z and LBank simultaneously overtook Binance — the world's largest exchange by genuine volume — on CoinMarketCap's adjusted trade volume rankings [2]:

- Bit-Z reached the **#1 position** on CoinMarketCap's adjusted volume ranking
- Binance dropped to #2
- LBank held the #3 position at the time of reporting

This ranking anomaly was immediately flagged by researchers because:
- No corresponding increase in web traffic, user registrations, or media coverage accompanied Bit-Z's ascent
- The simultaneous rise of two low-profile exchanges above Binance defied organic market dynamics
- The anomaly coincided with CoinMarketCap implementing new "adjusted volume" metrics — which both Bit-Z and LBank apparently gamed
- Rankings corrected quickly as CoinMarketCap refined its methodology

### Bitwise Asset Management SEC Presentation (March 2019)

Bitwise's analysis for the SEC identified only **10 exchanges** with genuine, verifiable Bitcoin trading volume: Binance, Bitfinex, bitFlyer, Bitstamp, Bittrex, Coinbase Pro, Gemini, itBit, Kraken, and Poloniex [3].

Bit-Z was not among these exchanges. Bitwise estimated that **95% of all reported Bitcoin trading volume** was fake, with exchanges like Bit-Z exemplifying the problem. The presentation demonstrated that exchanges fabricated volumes to:

- Attract **listing fee revenue** from token projects seeking visibility
- Gain **perceived legitimacy** through high CoinMarketCap rankings
- Drive **retail user deposits** from traders who equated high rankings with trustworthiness

### Blockchain Transparency Institute (December 2018)

BTI's December 2018 Market Surveillance Report quantified Bit-Z's volume inflation at **469 times** its estimated genuine volume, placing it among the worst offenders globally [4]. BTI's analysis cross-referenced reported trading data with order book depth, on-chain transaction patterns, and web traffic to estimate genuine activity levels. At its peak, Bit-Z reported daily trading volumes exceeding **$1.5 billion** — a figure irreconcilable with the exchange's actual user base and order book depth.

## Transaction Mining: Structural Incentives for Wash Trading

Bit-Z operated a "transaction mining" model — a mechanism prevalent among several exchanges in 2018–2019 that structurally incentivized self-trading [5]:

- Users received BZ tokens proportional to their trading fees paid
- When the token value exceeded the fees paid, wash trading became a net-positive activity
- This transformed trading from a cost into a revenue source, eliminating the primary economic deterrent against self-trading
- The model created a self-reinforcing cycle: wash trading generated tokens, tokens had market value, and the apparent volume attracted more listings and user deposits

The transaction mining model was not unique to Bit-Z — exchanges including FCoin, CoinBene, and BitMax adopted similar models — but the combination of transaction mining and aggressive volume inflation placed Bit-Z among the most egregious offenders in the 2018–2019 period.

## Closure (October 2021)

Following China's intensified cryptocurrency crackdown in 2021, Bit-Z announced its closure in October 2021:

- On October 8, 2021, Bit-Z issued a notice that it would cease all operations
- Users were given until October 21, 2021, to withdraw remaining assets
- The exchange offered to repurchase BZ tokens at a fixed rate of **0.16 USDT** per token
- The closure was part of a broader wave of China-linked exchange shutdowns following regulatory action that year, which also affected exchanges including Huobi's China operations

## Market Manipulation Implications

Bit-Z's case, paired with LBank's simultaneous rise, illustrates a coordinated pattern of aggregator manipulation:

1. **Volume fabrication** creates artificially high CoinMarketCap rankings
2. **High rankings** attract listing fees from token projects seeking visibility
3. **Listing fee revenue** funds continued wash trading operations
4. **Perceived legitimacy** attracts retail deposits, creating real revenue
5. The cycle repeats, with fabricated metrics driving genuine economic activity

The 0.79% genuine volume figure is particularly significant — it represents the lowest real-to-reported ratio documented among the exchanges studied by The Tie, suggesting that virtually all of Bit-Z's reported market activity existed solely as a mechanism for gaming aggregator rankings.

## Relevance to Market Health Metrics

Bit-Z's volume patterns demonstrate several indicators documented in the DN Institute [Market Health Metrics](https://dn.institute/research/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/) framework:

- **Volume-traffic divergence**: Reported trading volume vastly exceeded what web traffic and user metrics could support
- **Rankings anomalies**: Sudden ascent to #1 ranking without corresponding market events
- **Transaction mining correlation**: Volume patterns directly tied to token incentive programs
- **Cross-validation failure**: No independent analysis verified Bit-Z's volume as genuine
- **Simultaneous anomaly**: The paired ranking manipulation with LBank suggests either coordinated activity or exploitation of the same aggregator vulnerability
