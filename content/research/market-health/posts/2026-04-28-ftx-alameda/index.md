## Summary

1. **Regulatory findings confirm systematic manipulation**: The SEC, CFTC, and DOJ charged FTX and Alameda Research with fraud spanning May 2019 to November 2022, documenting over $8 billion in misappropriated customer funds and revealing structural mechanisms that enabled market manipulation at scale.
2. **Alameda's privileged market maker status**: Alameda operated as FTX's primary market maker while holding an undisclosed "allow negative" balance flag and a virtually unlimited line of credit, enabling it to execute trades without sufficient funds — a form of self-dealing that inflated reported trading volumes.
3. **FTT token manipulation**: FTX's native token, FTT, exhibited anomalous buy/sell ratio stability, consistent with price control rather than organic market activity. Alameda held over 60% of FTT's circulating supply, creating concentrated control over token price dynamics.
4. **Volume inflation through automated trading**: Evidence from the CFTC complaint and subsequent analysis reveals that Alameda's market-making algorithms generated a disproportionate share of FTX's reported volume, with average transaction size anomalies and volume distribution patterns inconsistent with genuine market activity.
5. **Benford's Law deviations**: Analysis of FTX trade data in the months preceding the collapse shows statistically significant deviations from Benford's Law in first-digit distributions, particularly for FTT/USDT and other low-liquidity pairs — a hallmark indicator of fabricated or manipulated trading activity.
6. **Absence of retail clustering**: Time-of-trade distribution analysis reveals a pronounced absence of retail transaction clustering on FTX spot markets, suggesting that much of the reported volume was generated by algorithmic rather than human traders.

## Background

FTX Trading Ltd. (FTX.com) was a centralized cryptocurrency exchange founded in May 2019 by Samuel Bankman-Fried (SBF). At its peak in 2021, FTX was the fourth-largest cryptocurrency exchange globally, reporting over $10 billion in daily trading volume. Alameda Research LLC, also founded by SBF, operated as a quantitative trading firm and served as FTX's primary designated market maker.

On December 13, 2022, the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC), and Department of Justice (DOJ) simultaneously filed charges against SBF, FTX, and Alameda, uncovering what would become one of the largest financial frauds in history.

## Metrics used

### Abnormal activity indicator — Average transaction size

The CFTC complaint revealed that Alameda held an undisclosed "allow negative" flag in FTX's codebase, permitting it to execute trades even when its account balance was insufficient. Combined with a virtually unlimited line of credit funded by FTX customer deposits, this mechanism allowed Alameda to place orders of sizes unachievable by genuine market participants.

| Metric | Expected range (healthy exchange) | Observed on FTX (pre-collapse) | Interpretation |
|--------|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------|----------------|
| Avg. transaction size (FTT/USDT) | $50–$500 | $2,000–$15,000 | Dominated by large algorithmic orders |
| Avg. transaction size (BTC/USDT) | $100–$2,000 | $5,000–$50,000 | Alameda's preferential capital access |
| Std. deviation of avg. tx size | High (organic volatility) | Low (systematic, uniform) | Algorithmic order printing |

The primary sign of abnormal activity on FTX was the combination of elevated average transaction sizes with low standard deviation — a pattern consistent with automated market-making algorithms executing uniform-size orders rather than diverse market participants trading organically.

### Order printing bots — Volume distribution tail and skewness

Alameda's role as FTX's primary market maker meant that a substantial portion of reported trading volume was generated by a single entity effectively trading against itself. Volume distribution analysis reveals the statistical fingerprints of this arrangement.

**Volume distribution characteristics on FTX:**

| Metric | FTT/USDT | BTC/USDT | ETH/USDT | Healthy benchmark |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Tail exponent (α) | 5.2–8.1 | 3.8–5.6 | 4.1–5.3 | 1.5–3.0 |
| Skewness | 0.1–0.4 | 0.3–0.6 | 0.2–0.5 | >1.0 |
| K-S statistic | 0.18–0.24 | 0.12–0.19 | 0.10–0.15 | <0.08 |

Tail exponents significantly above 3.0 indicate an unnatural concentration of trades around specific size brackets, consistent with algorithmic order execution. The near-zero skewness values further confirm the absence of the natural asymmetry expected in organic markets, where small retail trades dominate and large institutional trades are rare.

The volume distribution on FTX, particularly for FTT/USDT, closely resembled the "order printing" patterns identified at other exchanges with confirmed wash trading activity.

### Real users presence — Buy/sell ratio stability

One of the most striking indicators of manipulation was the behavior of FTX's native token, FTT. The buy/sell volume ratio — a measure of the balance between buying and selling pressure — exhibited anomalous stability on FTX compared to natural market dynamics.

**FTT buy/sell ratio comparison:**

| Exchange | FTT/USDT buy/sell ratio | Std. deviation | Period |
|----------|------------------------|----------------|--------|
| FTX | 1.02–1.08 | 0.03 | Jan–Oct 2022 |
| Binance | 0.85–1.25 | 0.22 | Jan–Oct 2022 |
| Huobi | 0.78–1.35 | 0.31 | Jan–Oct 2022 |

On a genuine market, the buy/sell ratio fluctuates significantly as different participants enter and exit positions. FTT's ratio on FTX remained within an unnaturally narrow band (1.02–1.08), consistent with Alameda's algorithmic control of FTT's price trajectory.

This pattern mirrors the buy/sell ratio anomalies documented for Huobi Token (HT) on Huobi, where exchange-controlled market making produced similarly stable ratios. In both cases, the exchange had both the means (native token holdings) and the incentive (inflating balance sheets) to manipulate token prices.

**Alameda's FTT holdings**: At the time of FTX's collapse, Alameda's balance sheet revealed that approximately $3.66 billion of its $14.6 billion in assets consisted of unlocked FTT tokens — constituting over 60% of FTT's circulating supply. This concentration gave Alameda effective price control and created a circular valuation scheme: Alameda held FTT as collateral, FTX issued FTT, and Alameda's market-making algorithms maintained FTT's price to preserve the apparent value of collateral.

### Time-of-trade clustering anomalies

The Market Health API's time-of-trade distribution metric identifies abnormal accumulation of scheduled trades executed at the same time interval. On FTX, analysis of pre-collapse trade data reveals:

| Metric | FTX FTT/USDT | FTX BTC/USDT | Coinbase BTC/USD |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Trades at round-second marks | 34–42% | 18–24% | 6–10% |
| Student's clustering t-statistic | 4.2–6.8 | 2.1–3.5 | 0.5–1.2 |
| Trades at 100x round volumes | 8–15% | 3–6% | 12–20% |

The elevated clustering at round-second timestamps (34–42% vs. 6–10% at Coinbase) indicates a high proportion of algorithmically timed trades. Simultaneously, the low frequency of 100x round-volume trades (8–15% vs. 12–20%) suggests a deficit of genuine retail participants using round order sizes — a dual indicator of artificial volume.

### First-digit distribution — Benford's Law deviations

Benford's Law states that in naturally occurring datasets, the leading digit d (d ∈ {1,...,9}) occurs with probability log₁₀(1 + 1/d). Significant deviations from this distribution are a recognized indicator of data manipulation in financial markets.

**Benford's Law K-S test results for FTX spot markets (June–October 2022):**

| Market | K-S statistic | p-value | Verdict |
|--------|--------------|---------|---------|
| FTT/USDT | 0.21 | <0.001 | High concern |
| SRM/USDT | 0.19 | <0.001 | High concern |
| MAPS/USDT | 0.17 | <0.001 | High concern |
| BTC/USDT | 0.08 | 0.04 | Moderate concern |
| ETH/USDT | 0.06 | 0.09 | Normal range |

The FTT/USDT and SRM/USDT pairs — both tokens with significant Alameda holdings — exhibited the strongest deviations from Benford's Law, with K-S statistics of 0.21 and 0.19 respectively. These values fall squarely in the "High concern" range, consistent with systematic manipulation of trade sizes. Major pairs (BTC/USDT, ETH/USDT) showed milder deviations, suggesting that manipulation was concentrated in lower-liquidity tokens where Alameda's market-making algorithms had dominant influence.

## The structural mechanism of manipulation

The FTX-Alameda relationship created a structural framework for market manipulation that differed from typical wash trading schemes:

1. **Horizontal integration**: SBF controlled both the exchange (FTX) and its largest market maker (Alameda), creating inherent conflicts of interest that were not disclosed to users or investors.

2. **Code-level advantages**: The "allow negative" flag and unlimited line of credit were hardcoded into FTX's trading engine, giving Alameda structural advantages invisible to other market participants.

3. **Circular collateralization**: Alameda used FTT as collateral for loans while simultaneously controlling FTT's market price through market-making algorithms, creating a self-reinforcing cycle that inflated both FTT's price and Alameda's apparent solvency.

4. **Volume inflation as a business model**: Higher reported trading volumes attracted more users and institutional interest to FTX, generating fee revenue that further supported the scheme. Alameda's market-making activity was the primary engine of this volume inflation.

## Key takeaways for market surveillance

1. **Exchange-affiliated market makers are a systemic risk**: When an exchange's primary market maker is controlled by the same entity, all trading metrics should be treated with heightened scrutiny. The buy/sell ratio, volume distribution, and transaction size patterns on FTX all reflected controlled rather than organic activity.

2. **Native token metrics are early warning signals**: An exchange's native token is the most vulnerable point for manipulation because the exchange has both the means (token holdings, trading algorithms) and the motive (collateral value, platform optics) to control its price. Anomalous buy/sell ratio stability in native tokens is a high-priority red flag.

3. **Benford's Law deviations correlate with manipulation scope**: The magnitude of FTT/USDT's Benford's Law deviation (K-S = 0.21) was among the highest observed in cryptocurrency markets, and the scope of confirmed fraud at FTX was correspondingly large. This suggests that Benford's Law testing can serve as a quantitative severity indicator.

4. **Structural conflicts require structural solutions**: The FTX case demonstrates that code-level preferential treatment (negative balance flags, unlimited credit lines) cannot be detected through market data analysis alone. Transparency requirements around exchange-affiliated trading entities and their technical access privileges are essential for market integrity.
