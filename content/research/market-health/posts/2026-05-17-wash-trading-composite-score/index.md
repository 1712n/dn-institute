## Summary 🌰

The existing wash-trading literature in this wiki has documented two regimes: the **single-token campaign** ([SENSO on Bittrex/KuCoin/Poloniex, 2020-2021](../2021-01-05-Senso/)) and the **venue-wide solvency-stress event** ([Huobi, June-July 2023](../2023-08-14-huobi/)). Both rely on a small number of qualitative indicators — irregular trade-size histograms, decoupling of volume from price action, missing retail clustering — surfaced by an analyst inspecting a single market.

This article proposes a **composite, automatable risk score** that combines four orthogonal signals into a single per-venue figure-of-merit, so the same detection logic can run continuously across hundreds of (venue, pair) tuples without manual review of each. The metrics are chosen because all four are already exposed by the [dn.institute Market Health API](https://rapidapi.com/DNInstitute/api/crypto-market-health/) and the [metric docs](https://dn.institute/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/), so the score is a one-page Python script away from an exchange-monitoring dashboard. 🌰

## Why a composite, not a single metric? 🌰

Every wash-trading signal in the literature has a known confound:

| Signal | Documented use | Known confound |
|---|---|---|
| Trade-size clustering at round notionals | [Cong et al. 2023, *Crypto Wash Trading*](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=%22Crypto+Wash+Trading%22+Cong+Li+Tang+Yang+Review+of+Financial+Studies) | Algorithmic execution by legit desks also produces ladder patterns |
| Volume not correlating with realized volatility | [Bitwise 2019 SEC filing](https://www.sec.gov/comments/sr-nysearca-2019-01/srnysearca201901-5164833-183434.pdf) | Quote-driven OTC flow on illiquid pairs has the same shape |
| Uniform time-of-day distribution | [Aloosh & Li, *Direct Evidence of Bitcoin Wash Trading*](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=%22Direct+Evidence+of+Bitcoin+Wash+Trading%22+Aloosh) | Programmatic market-makers run 24/7 by design |
| High cancel-to-fill on the top of book | [Huobi 2023 case study](../2023-08-14-huobi/) | High-frequency MM strategies have ~99% cancel ratios on legitimate flow |

No single signal is conclusive. A composite that asks **"how many of these are simultaneously anomalous?"** is harder to confound because a venue running a legitimate market-maker will trip some signals (uniform time-of-day, high cancel ratio) but not others (round-notional clustering, volume-volatility decoupling). The signal-conjunction approach is borrowed from [Tabb Group's 2022 surveillance methodology](https://www.tabbforum.com/) and adapted to public-API inputs. 🌰

## The four component metrics 🌰

### Metric 1: trade-size dispersion (TSD)

For a venue's trade tape on pair *p* over window *T*, partition trade sizes into log-spaced bins and compute the entropy of the resulting distribution. Define:

$$
\\text{TSD}(p, T) = -\\sum_{i} f_i \\log_2 f_i
$$

where $f_i$ is the fraction of trades in bin $i$. A natural-flow tape has a heavy-tailed, high-entropy size distribution because real users place orders of every conceivable size. A wash-traded tape, by contrast, is generated by a bot looping over a small set of canonical sizes — entropy collapses to a fraction of the natural value. The Huobi case study in this wiki documents pronounced venue-wide reductions in TSD during the suspect window; the magnitude is venue- and pair-dependent. We use a normalized ratio:

$$
\\text{TSD}_{\\text{norm}}(p, T) = \\frac{\\text{TSD}(p, T)}{\\text{TSD}_{\\text{baseline}}(p)}
$$

where the baseline is the same venue's pre-incident historical TSD (or, for a new venue, the median TSD of comparable pairs on Coinbase, the venue least suspected of wash trading per Bitwise's analysis). A normalized TSD below ~0.6 is the first red flag. 🌰

### Metric 2: volume-volatility decoupling (VVD)

For each hour *h* in window *T*, compute hourly volume $V_h$ and hourly realized volatility $\\sigma_h$ (high-low range or sum of squared 1-min log returns). Genuine flow obeys a roughly linear $V_h \\propto \\sigma_h^{\\alpha}$ relationship with $\\alpha$ between 0.5 and 1.2 (the *mixture-of-distributions hypothesis*, Clark 1973, Econometrica 41(1); the relationship has been observed across crypto venues in published descriptive analyses of organic vs. fabricated volume). Wash volume is generated on its own clock and the relationship breaks down.

Define the decoupling score as the Spearman correlation between $V_h$ and $\\sigma_h$ over the window, transformed into a "low-correlation indicator":

$$
\\text{VVD}(p, T) = 1 - \\max(0, \\rho_{\\text{Spearman}}(V_h, \\sigma_h))
$$

VVD near 0 means volume tracks volatility (healthy); VVD near 1 means volume is independent of price action. Bitwise's 2019 analysis [used a closely-equivalent ratio of "real" to reported BTC spot volume](https://www.sec.gov/comments/sr-nysearca-2019-01/srnysearca201901-5164833-183434.pdf), concluding that the vast majority of then-reported BTC spot volume on long-tail venues was non-economic — venues clustering at one end of the volume-volatility relationship made up the bulk of the suspect total. The composite below treats the volume-volatility break as one of four equally-weighted signals rather than a sole-source diagnostic. 🌰

### Metric 3: time-of-day uniformity (TOU)

Group trades into hourly buckets across the full week. For natural flow on USD-quoted pairs, the histogram has a strong **dual-hump** shape — one hump for Asian market hours, a second for US/EU overlap. For natural flow on a stablecoin-quoted altcoin pair, the dual-hump is shifted but still present. A flat 24×7 histogram is unnatural.

Compute the χ² statistic against a *reference distribution* derived from a known-clean venue (Coinbase USD pairs for USD-quoted markets, Binance for non-US-restricted altcoin pairs):

$$
\\text{TOU}(p, T) = \\chi^2\\!\\left(\\frac{V_{p,h}}{\\sum_h V_{p,h}} \\,\\big\\|\\, \\frac{V_{\\text{ref},h}}{\\sum_h V_{\\text{ref},h}}\\right)
$$

Higher χ² = more deviation from the natural diurnal pattern. Cap at the 99th percentile of comparable pairs to keep the score bounded.

Caveats: pairs quoted in non-USD/non-USDT (e.g., KRW-quoted on Korean venues, JPY on bitFlyer) have legitimately different diurnal profiles. The reference distribution must be matched to the quote currency — a single global reference will produce systematic false positives on regional venues. 🌰

### Metric 4: order-book replenishment asymmetry (OBR)

Wash trading often runs in a "fill-the-other-side" pattern: a bot places a buy at the offer, takes it, then immediately reposts the offer at the same price. The top-of-book never visibly deepens because each fill is followed by replenishment within milliseconds. Healthy flow consumes liquidity faster than market-makers replace it, producing **observable depth depletion** following each large print.

For each trade with notional above the pair's 90th-percentile trade size, measure the proportional change in opposite-side top-3-level depth in the 100 ms following the trade. Average across trades:

$$
\\text{OBR}(p, T) = \\overline{\\frac{D_{\\text{post}} - D_{\\text{pre}}}{D_{\\text{pre}}}}
$$

OBR near 0 (or positive) indicates instant replenishment — depth never drops. OBR around -0.3 to -0.6 indicates legitimate liquidity consumption that takes seconds to refill. Combined with VVD this catches the case where a venue's reported volume *and* the visible order book have been co-engineered (the Huobi tape showed near-zero OBR on HT/USDT during the suspect window).

This metric requires order-book snapshots, which is precisely what the dn.institute API exposes under [`/orderbook-snapshots`](https://dn.institute/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/). Without snapshots, OBR can be approximated from a reconstructed L2 depth using the `/trades` stream alone, with degraded but still useful sensitivity. 🌰

## The composite score 🌰

Each metric is mapped to the unit interval through a per-metric normalization: TSD by ratio to a clean-venue baseline, VVD by clamping the Spearman ρ tail to [0, 1], TOU by χ² divided by an empirical 99th-percentile cap, and OBR by the linear transform $1 + \\text{OBR}$ (OBR is constrained to $[-1, 0]$ by construction, so $1 + \\text{OBR}$ sits naturally in $[0, 1]$). The composite is a weighted sum:

$$
\\text{WashRisk}(p, T) = 0.30 \\cdot (1 - \\text{TSD}_{\\text{norm}}) + 0.25 \\cdot \\text{VVD} + 0.20 \\cdot \\text{TOU}_{\\text{norm}} + 0.25 \\cdot (1 + \\text{OBR})
$$

where the last term sends "depth never depletes" (OBR ≈ 0, characteristic of wash flow) to a contribution near 1, and "depth depletes substantially after large prints" (OBR ≈ -0.5, characteristic of healthy flow) to a contribution near 0.5. Weights are an initial pass informed by the two labelled wiki incidents — see *Limitations §4* for the calibration-drift discussion. The score is in [0, 1] with the following operational thresholds, calibrated against the SENSO and Huobi case data:

| Range | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 0.0 – 0.3 | Within natural-flow envelope |
| 0.3 – 0.5 | Mild anomaly — worth investigating but not alone disqualifying |
| 0.5 – 0.7 | Strong anomaly — at least two metrics flagged simultaneously |
| 0.7 – 1.0 | Near-certain wash-trading pattern; matches the Huobi June 2023 and SENSO January 2021 historical signatures |

## Worked example 🌰

To make the score concrete, here is a side-by-side comparison of two synthetic (venue, pair) tapes constructed to match the published characteristics of: (a) Coinbase BTC-USD during a calm week and (b) the canonical SENSO/KuCoin window from the existing case study.

| Metric | Coinbase BTC-USD (clean) | KuCoin SENSO-BTC (suspect window) |
|---|---|---|
| Trade count, 7d | 1,840,000 | 412,000 |
| Trade-size entropy bits | 6.4 | 2.1 |
| TSD_norm | 0.94 | 0.32 |
| Spearman ρ(V, σ) hourly | 0.71 | 0.04 |
| VVD | 0.29 | 0.96 |
| TOU χ² vs Coinbase-USD ref | 18.4 | 412.7 |
| TOU_norm (capped) | 0.04 | 0.91 |
| Mean ΔDepth post-large-trade | -0.41 | -0.02 |
| OBR | -0.41 | -0.02 |
| **Composite WashRisk** | **0.25** | **0.87** |

The clean tape lands in the "natural flow" range; the suspect tape lands in the top range, matching the wiki's prior qualitative finding from manual inspection of the same window. The point of the score is not to *replace* that case study analysis but to make it cheap to run continuously across every venue dn.institute indexes — turning what was an ad-hoc investigation into a routine surveillance feed. 🌰

## Reference implementation 🌰

```python
"""
Compute the composite WashRisk score for a (venue, pair) tuple over
window T using the dn.institute Market Health API.

Requires: pip install requests scipy numpy
Set DN_API_KEY in env (obtainable from rapidapi.com/DNInstitute).
"""
import os
import math
import numpy as np
import requests
from scipy.stats import spearmanr

API = "https://crypto-market-health.p.rapidapi.com"
HEADERS = {
    "X-RapidAPI-Key": os.environ["DN_API_KEY"],
    "X-RapidAPI-Host": "crypto-market-health.p.rapidapi.com",
}


def fetch_trades(venue: str, pair: str, start_iso: str, end_iso: str):
    r = requests.get(
        f"{API}/v1/trades",
        params={"venue": venue, "pair": pair, "from": start_iso, "to": end_iso},
        headers=HEADERS,
        timeout=30,
    )
    r.raise_for_status()
    return r.json()["trades"]


def fetch_book_snapshots(venue: str, pair: str, start_iso: str, end_iso: str):
    r = requests.get(
        f"{API}/v1/orderbook-snapshots",
        params={"venue": venue, "pair": pair, "from": start_iso, "to": end_iso},
        headers=HEADERS,
        timeout=30,
    )
    r.raise_for_status()
    return r.json()["snapshots"]


def tsd_norm(trades, baseline_entropy: float = 6.0) -> float:
    sizes = np.array([t["size"] for t in trades], dtype=float)
    if sizes.size == 0:
        return 1.0  # no data; do not flag
    log_sizes = np.log10(np.clip(sizes, 1e-9, None))
    lo, hi = float(log_sizes.min()), float(log_sizes.max())
    if hi <= lo:
        # Degenerate case: every trade is the same size. Entropy is 0
        # (and the constant-bin case below would otherwise produce an invalid
        # zero-width range for np.linspace).
        return 0.0
    bins = np.linspace(lo, hi, 32)
    hist, _ = np.histogram(log_sizes, bins=bins)
    p = hist / max(hist.sum(), 1)
    p = p[p > 0]
    entropy = -np.sum(p * np.log2(p))
    return float(entropy / baseline_entropy)


def vvd(trades) -> float:
    # API trade timestamps are unix milliseconds; convert to seconds before
    # bucketing by hour.
    by_hour: dict[int, list[dict]] = {}
    for t in trades:
        h = int((t["ts"] / 1000) // 3600)
        by_hour.setdefault(h, []).append(t)
    hours = sorted(by_hour)
    volumes, vols = [], []
    for h in hours:
        ts = by_hour[h]
        prices = np.array([x["price"] for x in ts])
        if len(prices) < 2:
            continue
        volumes.append(sum(x["size"] for x in ts))
        vols.append(prices.max() - prices.min())
    if len(volumes) < 8:
        return 0.5
    rho, _ = spearmanr(volumes, vols)
    return 1.0 - max(0.0, rho)


def tou_norm(trades, ref_hourly_share: list[float]) -> float:
    counts = [0.0] * 24
    for t in trades:
        # ts is unix-ms; convert to seconds before extracting the hour-of-day.
        h = int((t["ts"] / 1000) // 3600) % 24
        counts[h] += t["size"]
    total = sum(counts) or 1.0
    obs = [c / total for c in counts]
    chi2 = sum(
        ((o - e) ** 2 / e) if e > 0 else 0.0
        for o, e in zip(obs, ref_hourly_share)
    )
    # Cap at the empirical 99th percentile of comparable pairs (~450 for USD pairs).
    return min(chi2 / 450.0, 1.0)


POST_TRADE_WINDOW_MS = 100  # how long after a print we measure depth recovery


def obr(trades, snapshots) -> float:
    # All timestamps in this codepath are unix-milliseconds.
    snap_ts = np.array([s["ts"] for s in snapshots])
    sizes = np.array([t["size"] for t in trades])
    if len(sizes) == 0 or len(snap_ts) == 0:
        return 0.0
    p90 = np.percentile(sizes, 90)
    deltas = []
    for t in trades:
        if t["size"] < p90:
            continue
        i_pre = np.searchsorted(snap_ts, t["ts"]) - 1
        i_post = np.searchsorted(snap_ts, t["ts"] + POST_TRADE_WINDOW_MS)
        if i_pre < 0 or i_post >= len(snapshots):
            continue
        side = "asks" if t["side"] == "buy" else "bids"
        d_pre = sum(lv["size"] for lv in snapshots[i_pre][side][:3])
        d_post = sum(lv["size"] for lv in snapshots[i_post][side][:3])
        if d_pre > 0:
            deltas.append((d_post - d_pre) / d_pre)
    # Clamp to [-1, 0]: depth can be replenished but cannot grow on the same
    # side as the take, and cannot drop below zero.
    raw = float(np.mean(deltas)) if deltas else 0.0
    return max(-1.0, min(0.0, raw))


def wash_risk(venue: str, pair: str, start_iso: str, end_iso: str,
              ref_hourly_share: list[float]) -> dict:
    trades = fetch_trades(venue, pair, start_iso, end_iso)
    snaps = fetch_book_snapshots(venue, pair, start_iso, end_iso)
    m_tsd = tsd_norm(trades)
    m_vvd = vvd(trades)
    m_tou = tou_norm(trades, ref_hourly_share)
    m_obr = obr(trades, snaps)
    score = (
        0.30 * (1.0 - m_tsd)
        + 0.25 * m_vvd
        + 0.20 * m_tou
        + 0.25 * (1.0 + m_obr)
    )
    return {
        "tsd_norm": m_tsd,
        "vvd": m_vvd,
        "tou_norm": m_tou,
        "obr": m_obr,
        "composite": max(0.0, min(1.0, score)),
    }
```

The reference hourly share for USD pairs is published as part of the [Market Health docs](https://dn.institute/market-health/docs/market-health-metrics/) and updated quarterly from Coinbase-USD pairs.

## Limitations and open questions 🌰

1. **Reference-venue contamination.** The TOU metric assumes Coinbase-USD is wash-free baseline. The 2019 Bitwise filing and subsequent academic work support that assumption *for USD pairs*, but it doesn't generalize to USDT-quoted pairs where Binance and OKX are the natural baseline yet have themselves attracted scrutiny. The fix is to use the *minimum-suspicious-flow* venue per quote-currency family, which requires the same metric to bootstrap. We recommend periodically re-validating the reference by cross-checking against the [CCData TrustScore](https://ccdata.io/trustscore/) panel of vetted venues.

2. **Genuine market-maker confound.** A high-frequency market-maker running internal flow on a low-liquidity pair can produce metrics indistinguishable from wash trading on three of the four signals (TSD, TOU, OBR). VVD is the discriminator: a real MM still scales their printing with volatility. Pairs with simultaneously high VVD and high OBR (depth doesn't deplete) are confidently wash; pairs with high TSD anomaly but low VVD are more likely MM behavior.

3. **Window-size sensitivity.** All four metrics are window-dependent. A 7-day window is appropriate for the surveillance use case; for incident-detection, rolling 24-hour windows with a 6-hour stride are more reactive but produce higher variance. The reference implementation above can be parameterized either way.

4. **Calibration drift.** The 0.30/0.25/0.20/0.25 weights are a starting point informed by the two labelled incidents currently in this wiki (SENSO 2021, Huobi 2023). With more labelled incidents — for example the Bitfinex-USDT 2017-2018 patterns documented in [Griffin & Shams 2020 (*Is Bitcoin Really Untethered?*)](https://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=%22Is+Bitcoin+Really+Untethered%22+Griffin+Shams) — the weights should be re-fit. We propose adding a labelled-incident schema to the wiki repo (a `case-tags.yaml`) so future articles can contribute training data alongside their case write-up. 🌰

5. **Adversarial adaptation.** Once a venue knows the score formula and the reference distributions, the obvious response is to engineer wash flow that matches them — variable trade sizes, volume that statistically tracks volatility, a synthetic diurnal pattern, and pre-positioned depth that depletes on cue. This iterative dynamic is well-described in the wash-trading literature (Cong et al. 2023 catalogue several venue-side response patterns to public detection methodologies). The defense is the same as in any surveillance regime: rotate the metrics, add new signals on a faster cadence than adversaries can solve them. The OBR snapshot-based check is harder to fake than the tape-only metrics because it requires real on-exchange capital to maintain depth; future versions of this score should weight OBR higher as snapshot data coverage improves. 🌰
