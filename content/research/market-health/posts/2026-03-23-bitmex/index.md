## Summary

1. **DOJ and CFTC Charges:** On October 1, 2020, the DOJ and CFTC simultaneously charged BitMEX and its co-founders — Arthur Hayes (CEO), Ben Delo (Chairman), and Samuel Reed (CTO) — with violating the Bank Secrecy Act by operating without AML/KYC programs and with illegally offering derivatives to U.S. customers.
2. **$100 Million Settlement:** BitMEX agreed to pay $100 million to resolve both the CFTC civil enforcement action and FinCEN compliance failures — at the time, one of the largest penalties imposed on a crypto exchange.
3. **Deliberate Regulatory Avoidance:** Internal communications revealed that BitMEX executives discussed establishing offshore structures specifically to avoid U.S. regulatory oversight, while knowingly serving U.S. customers who generated substantial trading volume.
4. **Market Impact:** BitMEX's leveraged trading products (up to 100x) played a significant role in crypto market volatility, with cascading liquidations during the March 2020 crash wiping out over $1 billion in trader positions within hours.
5. **Founder Sentences:** Hayes was sentenced to six months of home detention and two years of probation. Reed was sentenced to probation. Delo received probation after paying a $10 million FinCEN fine.

## Background

BitMEX (Bitcoin Mercantile Exchange), founded in 2014 by Arthur Hayes, Ben Delo, and Samuel Reed, pioneered cryptocurrency derivatives trading, particularly perpetual swap contracts. Operating from the Seychelles through the entity 100x Group (formerly HDR Global Trading), BitMEX became one of the largest crypto derivatives platforms, processing over $1 trillion in trading volume. The platform offered leverage of up to 100x on Bitcoin futures, making it both enormously profitable and a source of systemic risk in crypto markets.

## Metrics used

### Absence of AML/KYC controls

BitMEX operated for approximately six years without implementing basic anti-money laundering controls:

- No customer identity verification was required to open an account or trade — only an email address was needed.
- No suspicious activity reports (SARs) were filed with FinCEN despite processing billions in transactions.
- The platform was accessible to users in sanctioned jurisdictions including Iran and Syria.
- BitMEX did not register as a futures commission merchant (FCM) with the CFTC despite offering derivatives to U.S. customers.

### Leveraged trading and liquidation cascades

BitMEX's 100x leverage products created systemic risks:

- During the **March 12-13, 2020 crash** ("Black Thursday"), Bitcoin dropped from approximately $7,900 to $3,800 — a 52% decline within 24 hours.
- BitMEX experienced over **$1 billion in liquidations** during this event, with cascading margin calls amplifying the price decline.
- The exchange went offline for approximately 25 minutes during the crash due to what BitMEX described as a "hardware issue" — critics alleged the downtime may have been deliberate to halt the liquidation cascade.
- Academic research has shown that BitMEX's liquidation engine, which aggressively liquidated underwater positions and used the proceeds to fund an insurance fund, contributed to price instability.

### Deliberate offshore structuring

Internal communications cited in the DOJ indictment revealed:

- Hayes discussed the benefits of operating from jurisdictions with minimal regulatory oversight.
- Executives acknowledged that U.S. users represented a significant portion of their user base.
- BitMEX implemented superficial geo-restrictions (easily bypassed via VPN) to maintain the appearance of excluding U.S. users while continuing to profit from their trading activity.

## Regulatory actions and legal outcomes

### CFTC enforcement — October 1, 2020

The CFTC filed a civil enforcement action charging BitMEX, Hayes, Delo, Reed, and Gregory Dwyer (Head of Business Development) with operating an unregistered trading platform and violating anti-money laundering regulations. The CFTC alleged that BitMEX illegally offered leveraged crypto trading to U.S. customers without registering as a swap execution facility or designated contract market.

### DOJ criminal charges — October 1, 2020

On the same day, the DOJ's Southern District of New York charged all four individuals with violating the Bank Secrecy Act. Samuel Reed was arrested the same day. Hayes voluntarily surrendered to authorities in April 2021.

### BitMEX settlement — August 2021

BitMEX agreed to pay **$100 million** to settle both the CFTC civil action and FinCEN administrative proceedings:

- **$50 million** to the CFTC
- **$50 million** to FinCEN (the largest FinCEN penalty against a virtual currency exchange at the time)

### Individual outcomes

| Defendant | Plea | Sentence | Additional |
|--|--|--|--|
| Arthur Hayes | Guilty (Feb 2022) | 6 months home detention + 2 years probation | $10M FinCEN fine |

| Samuel Reed | Guilty (Mar 2022) | Probation | — |

All four defendants pleaded guilty to one count of violating the Bank Secrecy Act.
