## Summary

1. **DOJ Indictment:** In October 2024, the DOJ charged Gotbit Consulting LLC and its CEO Aleksei Andriunin with wire fraud and market manipulation conspiracy as part of a broad FBI operation targeting crypto market makers engaged in wash trading and artificial volume inflation.
2. **FBI Sting Operation (Token-1):** The FBI created a fake cryptocurrency token (NexFundAI/Token-1) specifically to expose market manipulation services. Gotbit was one of several firms that agreed to provide wash trading services for the token, believing it to be a legitimate client engagement.
3. **Systematic Wash Trading Service:** Gotbit operated a market-making business that explicitly offered wash trading as a service to token issuers, using custom bots to generate fictitious trading volume across multiple exchanges to inflate token rankings on platforms like CoinMarketCap and CoinGecko.
4. **18 Defendants, 4 Companies:** The broader DOJ action ("Operation Token Mirrors") charged 18 individuals and 4 entities across multiple market-making firms, representing one of the largest coordinated crypto fraud enforcement actions.
5. **$25 Million Seized:** Authorities seized approximately $25 million in cryptocurrency and deactivated trading bots responsible for wash trading across more than 60 tokens.

## Background

Gotbit Consulting LLC, founded by Aleksei Andriunin (a Russian national based in Portugal), operated as a cryptocurrency market-making firm. While legitimate market makers provide liquidity by placing real buy and sell orders, Gotbit's primary business was selling artificial volume — executing wash trades to inflate the apparent trading activity of client tokens. This service was marketed openly to token issuers who needed higher trading volumes to gain exchange listings, attract investors, and improve rankings on crypto data aggregators.

## Metrics used

### The wash trading service model

Gotbit's business model, as described in the DOJ indictment and FBI evidence, operated as follows:

- Token issuers (often meme coin or DeFi projects) contracted Gotbit to "make markets" for their tokens.
- Gotbit deployed **automated trading bots** that simultaneously placed buy and sell orders for the same token using multiple wallets controlled by Gotbit — classic wash trading.
- The bots were configured to generate specific volume targets, creating the appearance of organic trading activity.
- Gotbit charged fees based on the volume generated and the duration of the engagement.
- The inflated volume caused tokens to rank higher on CoinMarketCap and CoinGecko, attracting real retail investors who relied on these rankings as signals of legitimate market activity.

### FBI undercover operation ("Token-1" / NexFundAI)

The FBI's investigation used a novel approach:

- FBI agents created a fake cryptocurrency token called **NexFundAI** (internally referred to as "Token-1") on the Ethereum blockchain.
- Undercover agents posing as token promoters approached multiple market-making firms, including Gotbit, requesting wash trading services.
- Gotbit agreed to provide wash trading for the FBI's token, demonstrating their standard operating procedures.
- The FBI recorded communications in which Gotbit representatives openly discussed generating artificial volume and manipulating markets.
- This evidence formed the basis for criminal charges against Gotbit and other firms involved in the sting.

### Scale of the operation

The broader "Operation Token Mirrors" revealed the extent of crypto market manipulation:

- **18 individuals and 4 entities** charged across multiple firms.
- Over **60 tokens** were identified as having been subject to wash trading by the charged market makers.
- Approximately **$25 million** in cryptocurrency was seized.
- Trading bots operated by the defendants were deactivated.
- The operation involved coordination between the FBI, DOJ, SEC, and IRS Criminal Investigation.

## Regulatory actions and legal outcomes

### DOJ — Operation Token Mirrors (October 2024)

On October 9, 2024, the DOJ announced charges against Gotbit and Aleksei Andriunin as part of the broader "Operation Token Mirrors" enforcement action. The charges included:

- Wire fraud conspiracy
- Market manipulation conspiracy

Andriunin was arrested in Portugal. Extradition proceedings were initiated.

### SEC parallel actions

The SEC filed parallel civil charges against multiple defendants in the operation, alleging securities fraud and market manipulation in connection with the wash trading of tokens that constituted securities.

### Other firms charged

The DOJ action also charged:

- **ZM Quant** — a market-making firm based in Asia
- **CLS Global** — a UAE-based market maker
- **MyTrade** — another firm providing wash trading services

Multiple individual employees of these firms were also charged.
