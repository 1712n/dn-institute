## Summary

1. U.S. regulators described John McAfee's late-2017 and early-2018 "Coin of the Day" and "Coin of the Week" promotions as a repeatable crypto scalping scheme: build an undisclosed position, publish a high-reach endorsement, then sell into the price reaction.
2. The CFTC complaint identifies Verge, Reddcoin, and Dogecoin as examples of this pattern, with pre-promotion purchases measured in bitcoin and rapid post-promotion exits.
3. The Reddcoin and Dogecoin examples provide particularly useful market-health indicators: large pre-event accumulation, an immediate public catalyst, sharp short-term price movement, and quick liquidation by accounts connected to the promoter.
4. DOJ alleged that the McAfee team made more than $2 million from altcoin scalping and more than $11 million from a related ICO touting scheme. The SEC separately alleged more than $23 million in undisclosed ICO compensation.
5. For market surveillance, the case shows why influencer-driven token moves should be analyzed as trade events, not just as communications events. The critical signal is the relationship between pre-event positioning, public promotion, and post-event distribution.

## Why This Case Matters For Market Health

The McAfee matter is not a wash-trading case. There is no need to show matched self-trades or fabricated exchange volume. Instead, the alleged manipulation came from a public figure using information asymmetry and social reach to create temporary demand in thinly traded digital assets.

That distinction makes the case useful for a broader market-manipulation wiki. Many crypto markets can be distorted without fake prints on an order book. If a promoter secretly accumulates supply, represents a recommendation as unbiased, and then exits while followers buy, the price chart may show real trades but still reflect a manipulated catalyst.

The CFTC treated bitcoin, ether, verge, dogecoin, reddcoin, and similar virtual currencies as commodities in interstate commerce for purposes of the complaint. The agency also called the action its first manipulative-scheme case involving digital assets. That makes it an early enforcement template for social-media-driven crypto market abuse.

## Manipulation Pattern

The public filings describe a four-step pattern:

| Stage           | Behavior                                                                                                              | Market-health signal                                                                           |
| --------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Asset selection | McAfee, Watson, and associates selected assets they believed McAfee's account could move.                             | Small or mid-cap token with high retail attention and limited liquidity.                       |
| Position build  | The team accumulated tokens before the public endorsement.                                                            | Buyer concentration before a scheduled or hinted social-media event.                           |
| Public pump     | McAfee promoted the token as a long-term recommendation while allegedly concealing the team's position and exit plan. | Price and volume spike tightly clustered after a tweet or interview.                           |
| Dump            | The team sold into the demand created by the promotion.                                                               | Rapid post-event distribution from wallets or exchange accounts connected to pre-event buyers. |

This pattern is different from normal market commentary. The manipulative feature is not merely that a recommendation moved price. It is that the recommendation was allegedly presented as independent while the promoter and associates had already positioned themselves to profit from the short-term reaction.

The examples show why event-window analysis is important. Reddcoin's approximately 100% move after the endorsement is not enough by itself to prove manipulation; crypto assets can move violently for ordinary reasons. The stronger signal is the sequence: acquisition shortly before the recommendation, a public catalyst from an account with hundreds of thousands of followers, and near-immediate selling by the same network.

## ICO Touting As A Parallel Channel

The SEC complaint describes a related but distinct channel of misconduct involving seven ICOs. According to the SEC, McAfee promoted at least seven offerings from November 2017 through February 2018 without disclosing compensation from the issuers. The complaint says those ICOs raised about $41 million and that McAfee received about $23.2 million in undisclosed compensation, including bitcoin, ether, and promoted tokens.

The SEC's tables give a compact view of the incentive problem:

| Category       | SEC allegation                                                                       |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| ETH payments   | About $11.0 million in ETH value received by McAfee across the seven touted ICOs.    |
| BTC payments   | About $644,000 in BTC value received for two of the touted ICOs.                     |
| Token payments | About $11.5 million in promoted-token value received across four of the touted ICOs. |
| Public reach   | At least forty tweets and replies touting the seven ICOs.                            |

The ICO portion is relevant to market-health analysis because the same information-asymmetry pattern appears: public recommendations looked independent, but private compensation gave the promoter a direct economic stake in investor demand.

## Surveillance Indicators

Market operators and researchers can treat this case as a checklist for detecting social-media scalping:

1. **Event source concentration:** Identify posts from accounts whose prior recommendations repeatedly correlate with short-term token moves.
2. **Pre-event accumulation:** Search for unusual buy-side concentration in the hours or days before a public promotion.
3. **Immediate price-volume response:** Compare the post-promotion return and volume spike with the token's normal intraday volatility and liquidity.
4. **Rapid distribution:** Look for selling from the same exchange accounts, wallet clusters, or related counterparties that accumulated before the promotion.
5. **Repeated pattern:** One profitable recommendation may be ambiguous. Repetition across assets, especially with similar timing and exits, is a stronger manipulation signal.
6. **Disclosure mismatch:** Compare public statements about independence, research, and long-term value with private compensation, affiliate ties, or observed trading.

These indicators work best together. A tweet-driven price spike can be organic. A pre-tweet wallet build can also be innocent. The market-health concern rises when the same actor or network both creates the public signal and exits into the demand that follows.

## Market-Health Takeaways

The McAfee case shows a manipulation mode that standard wash-trading metrics may miss. Trade count, volume distribution, and self-trade detection remain important, but they should be paired with event-driven analysis when a token's primary catalyst is a high-reach influencer.

For thinly traded assets, a single public endorsement can function like a liquidity shock. If that shock is paired with concealed pre-positioning and immediate liquidation, the post-event price does not represent neutral price discovery. It reflects the market impact of a manufactured signal.
