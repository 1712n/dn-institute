## Summary

On January 23, 2025, [Phemex](https://phemex.com/), a Singapore-based cryptocurrency exchange, suffered a security breach in which an attacker drained its hot wallets across sixteen blockchains, including Ethereum, Solana, XRP, Bitcoin, Tron and BNB Chain. Phemex [detected the intrusion at 11:30 UTC](https://phemex.com/announcements/phemex-hot-wallet-security-incident-update-and-timeline) and suspended withdrawals the same day. Blockchain security firm [Cyvers first flagged about $29 million leaving the hot wallets](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2025/01/23/crypto-exchange-phemex-investigating-hack-reports-as-usd29m-drained-from-hot-wallets), but as the draining continued the tally rose: [PeckShield put the figure near $69 million](https://therecord.media/69-million-stolen-cyberattack-crypto-platform-phemex) and [Halborn and others settled on roughly $73 million](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-phemex-hack-january-2025), making it the largest crypto-exchange hack of 2025 until the Bybit breach weeks later. The attacker [went after the biggest balances first and converted the stablecoins to ETH, which no issuer can freeze](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-phemex-hack-january-2025), staying ahead of any blacklist. Phemex's cold wallets were untouched and the exchange pledged to cover user losses, but the breach points to a serious hot-wallet and key-management failure at a multi-chain exchange. Independent investigators later [tied the funds on-chain to the Lazarus Group](https://cointelegraph.com/news/lazarus-group-consolidates-bybit-phemex-hacker-wallet), the North Korean state-sponsored group behind many of the largest exchange thefts.

## Attackers

The breach has been attributed to North Korea's Lazarus Group. In the immediate aftermath the operators were not identified, though [the scale and execution of the theft were consistent with North Korea's Lazarus Group](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-steal-85-million-worth-of-cryptocurrency-from-phemex/). The link hardened a month later: after the $1.5 billion Bybit hack of February 2025, which [the FBI attributed to North Korean actors it tracks as "TraderTraitor"](https://www.ic3.gov/psa/2025/psa250226), on-chain investigator ZachXBT [showed that the Bybit attacker consolidated funds into a wallet used in the Phemex hack](https://cointelegraph.com/news/lazarus-group-consolidates-bybit-phemex-hacker-wallet), placing the two incidents in the same cluster. Because the FBI has formally tied the Bybit theft to North Korea, the shared wallet is strong evidence that the same operators drained Phemex; the rapid multi-chain sweep also matches Lazarus's laundering pattern in the [May 2024 DMM Bitcoin hack](https://www.merklescience.com/blog/hack-track-dmm-flow-of-funds-analysis).

The attackers operated the following Ethereum addresses:

- Consolidation wallet [0x5B34414e95a8b8D0B16a39BAf5b97CEc1d517E22](https://etherscan.io/address/0x5B34414e95a8b8D0B16a39BAf5b97CEc1d517E22).
- Stablecoin receivers [0x17BCC630B1409637D42dFb278f8E2ea9fc862631](https://etherscan.io/address/0x17BCC630B1409637D42dFb278f8E2ea9fc862631) and [0x6C42F03d730b7643939fA1D00416cB2985eD9cF3](https://etherscan.io/address/0x6C42F03d730b7643939fA1D00416cB2985eD9cF3).

## Losses

Estimates of the loss grew over the first 48 hours as forensic firms reconciled activity across chains, and because some movements were Phemex itself relocating remaining funds to safety. [Cyvers' initial figure was about $29 million](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2025/01/23/crypto-exchange-phemex-investigating-hack-reports-as-usd29m-drained-from-hot-wallets); [PeckShield raised it to about $69 million](https://therecord.media/69-million-stolen-cyberattack-crypto-platform-phemex); [Halborn, Hacken and Merkle Science converged on roughly $73 million](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-phemex-hack-january-2025); and [MetaMask's Taylor Monahan calculated at least $85 million](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hackers-steal-85-million-worth-of-cryptocurrency-from-phemex/) by the weekend.

A [forensic breakdown by chain](https://ashourics.medium.com/phemex-breach-analysis-a-multi-chain-hot-wallet-heist-dd16f9aeb645) put the largest losses on:

- Ethereum: about $20.4 million
- Solana: about $17.0 million
- XRP: about $13.5 million

with smaller amounts across Bitcoin, BNB Chain, Avalanche, Tron, Polygon, Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, Litecoin, Dogecoin and Cardano. The exchange's [proof of reserves showed roughly $461 million in cold wallets](https://www.merklescience.com/blog/hack-track-phemex-flow-of-funds-analysis), so the theft was around 15 percent of its total assets. Phemex said affected user balances would be covered.

## Timeline

- **January 23, 2025, 11:30 UTC**: Phemex [detects unusual activity in its hot wallet](https://phemex.com/announcements/phemex-hot-wallet-security-incident-update-and-timeline), triggers its emergency response, isolates the affected devices and notifies third-party security firms and law enforcement; [Cyvers independently flags about $29 million draining across chains](https://www.coindesk.com/business/2025/01/23/crypto-exchange-phemex-investigating-hack-reports-as-usd29m-drained-from-hot-wallets).
- **January 23, 2025, around 12:00 UTC**: the attacker's consolidation wallet moves stolen stablecoins out, including [744,696 USDT](https://etherscan.io/tx/0x933b055792a06ba92af2f50af8d57a0b8c190eb2e75a75c6e35a2caa206a8bb2) and [1,767,957 USDC](https://etherscan.io/tx/0x8f59639866caa7bfe81687bb5a95a8fb800105d5a724bfee3debe5b833cefde7), [swaps them into ETH to dodge an issuer freeze](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-phemex-hack-january-2025) and routes the proceeds [through mixers such as Tornado Cash](https://cointelegraph.com/news/lazarus-group-consolidates-bybit-phemex-hacker-wallet); the laundering spans [more than 125 transactions](https://securrtech.medium.com/the-phemex-exchange-exploit-a-deep-dive-into-the-37-million-hack-af82bcb5cd0d) across the affected chains.
- **January 23, 2025, 15:13 UTC**: Phemex [suspends withdrawals on all chains and publishes a proof of reserves](https://phemex.com/announcements/phemex-hot-wallet-security-incident-update-and-timeline).
- **January 23-24, 2025**: the draining continues; [PeckShield raises the estimate to about $69 million](https://therecord.media/69-million-stolen-cyberattack-crypto-platform-phemex) and [Rekt publishes a flow-of-funds writeup](https://rekt.news/phemex-rekt/).
- **January 24-26, 2025**: Phemex [restores withdrawals in stages under manual review](https://phemex.com/announcements/phemex-hot-wallet-security-incident-update-and-timeline), beginning with ETH, USDT and USDC on Ethereum (Jan 24, 17:46 UTC), then Bitcoin and Solana (Jan 25), then Arbitrum, Optimism, BNB Chain, Polygon and Base (Jan 26).
- **February 2025**: Phemex [reports all withdrawal services restored](https://phemex.com/announcements/phemex-hot-wallet-security-incident-update-and-timeline); after the Bybit hack, [ZachXBT connects the Bybit attacker on-chain to the Phemex hacker wallet](https://cointelegraph.com/news/lazarus-group-consolidates-bybit-phemex-hacker-wallet), strengthening the Lazarus attribution.
- **December 2025**: [SlowMist's 2025 annual security report](https://lazarus.day/incidents/phemex) lists Phemex among the year's Lazarus-linked exchange breaches.

## Security Failure Causes

- **Over-funded hot wallets.** Sixteen chains' worth of withdrawable balances, on the order of tens of millions of dollars, were reachable from internet-connected hot wallets rather than held in cold storage, so a single compromise exposed the whole float.
- **Access-control and key-management failure.** Investigators characterized the breach as an [access-control failure rather than a smart-contract exploit](https://ashourics.medium.com/phemex-breach-analysis-a-multi-chain-hot-wallet-heist-dd16f9aeb645), most likely from compromised private keys, a leaked API key, or insider access to wallet infrastructure. That all sixteen chains fell in quick succession points to shared or co-located signing material rather than sixteen independent compromises.
- **Multi-chain single point of failure.** Supporting many blockchains multiplied the attack surface: each chain needs its own key handling, and the attacker [moved from one chain to the next faster than the team could lock them down](https://rekt.news/phemex-rekt/).
- **Weak withdrawal controls.** The speed and size of the outflows suggest the absence of effective per-asset withdrawal rate limits or anomaly-triggered freezes that could have contained the loss.

The standard remedies map directly to these failures: most reserves in cold storage, multi-party computation custody in place of single-key hot wallets, and automated withdrawal limits with anomaly detection. Phemex says it isolated the affected systems and [rebuilt its custody around multi-party computation and tiered cold storage](https://phemex.com/blogs/security-review-2026-transparent-cex).
