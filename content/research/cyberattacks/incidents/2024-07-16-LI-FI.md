## Summary

On July 16, 2024, [LI.FI Protocol](https://li.fi/), a cross-chain bridging and DEX aggregation platform, was exploited for approximately [$11.6 million](https://li.fi/knowledge-hub/incident-report-16th-july/) in USDC, USDT, and DAI. The attack targeted a newly deployed smart contract facet that lacked proper call validation, allowing the attacker to make arbitrary calls to any contract and drain funds from 153 wallets that had granted infinite token approvals to the LI.FI contract. The exploit occurred on both Ethereum and Arbitrum chains within minutes of the vulnerable facet's deployment.

## Attackers

The identity of the attacker remains unknown. The attacker used the following addresses:

- **Ethereum**: [0x8B3Cb6Bf982798fba233Bca56749e22EEc42DcF3](https://etherscan.io/address/0x8B3Cb6Bf982798fba233Bca56749e22EEc42DcF3) (exploiter)
- The vulnerable facet was deployed at [0xf28A352377663cA134bd27B582b1a9A4dad7e534](https://etherscan.io/address/0xf28A352377663cA134bd27B582b1a9A4dad7e534) on both Ethereum and Arbitrum

## Losses

- Approximately $11.6 million total stolen
  - USDC, USDT, and DAI stablecoins drained
  - 153 wallets affected across [Ethereum](https://etherscan.io/address/0xf28A352377663cA134bd27B582b1a9A4dad7e534) and [Arbitrum](https://arbiscan.io/address/0xf28A352377663cA134bd27B582b1a9A4dad7e534)
- Only wallets with **infinite token approvals** to the LI.FI contract were affected; wallets using finite approvals (the default setting) were not impacted

## Timeline

- **July 16, 2024**: A new smart contract facet is deployed to the LI.FI diamond proxy contract on Ethereum and Arbitrum
- **July 16, 2024**: Within minutes of deployment, the attacker exploits the [missing validation check](https://github.com/lifinance/contracts/pull/700) in the new facet to drain funds from wallets with infinite approvals
- **July 16, 2024**: [LI.FI team detects](https://li.fi/knowledge-hub/incident-report-16th-july/) the breach and activates their incident response plan, disabling the vulnerable facet across all chains to prevent further unauthorized access
- **July 16, 2024**: Security researchers including [@pcaversaccio](https://x.com/pcaversaccio) and [Hexagate](https://x.com/hexagate_) assist in identifying and containing the exploit
- **July 16, 2024**: LI.FI [publicly warns users](https://x.com/laborXapp/status/1813245498406437025) to revoke approvals for the affected contract address
- **July 18, 2024**: LI.FI publishes a [detailed incident report](https://li.fi/knowledge-hub/incident-report-16th-july/) confirming the $11.6 million loss and disclosing the root cause as a human error in the deployment review process
- **July 18, 2024**: LI.FI announces, with backing from its major investors, that it is [evaluating options to fully compensate affected users](https://li.fi/knowledge-hub/incident-report-16th-july/)

## Security Failure Causes

- **Missing call validation in new facet**: The LI.FI contract uses a [diamond proxy pattern](https://eips.ethereum.org/EIPS/eip-2535) with multiple facets. The LibSwap library allows the contract to make calls to external DEXs and other entities, normally validated against a whitelist of approved contract addresses and functions. The newly deployed facet was missing this validation check entirely, allowing the attacker to [make arbitrary calls to any contract](https://li.fi/knowledge-hub/incident-report-16th-july/) without restriction.
- **Deployment process oversight failure**: According to LI.FI's incident report, the missing validation was caused by "an individual human error in overseeing the deployment process." Despite having a multi-step deployment review process, the critical security check was not caught before the facet went live.
- **Recurring vulnerability pattern**: LI.FI had suffered a [similar exploit in March 2022](https://blog.li.fi/20th-march-the-exploit-e9e0c9c03004/) involving the same class of vulnerability — unvalidated arbitrary calls through the swap function. The fact that a nearly identical bug was reintroduced two years later points to insufficient automated security checks and regression testing in the deployment pipeline.
- **Infinite approval risk**: The exploit only affected users who had granted infinite token approvals, a common but risky practice in DeFi that gives a smart contract unlimited spending authority over a user's tokens. This amplified the impact of the vulnerability.
