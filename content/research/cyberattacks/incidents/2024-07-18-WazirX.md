## Summary

On July 18, 2024, [WazirX](https://wazirx.com/), then one of India's largest cryptocurrency exchanges, [lost about $230 million](https://therecord.media/wazirx-crypto-platform-confirms-230-million-heist) (some estimates put it at $234.9 million) from an Ethereum [Gnosis Safe](https://safe.global/) multisignature wallet held under a custody arrangement with [Liminal Custody](https://www.liminalcustody.com/).

- The wallet used a [4-of-6 signature scheme](https://www.halborn.com/blog/post/explained-the-wazirx-hack-july-2024): five keys held by WazirX on Ledger hardware wallets, one key held by Liminal, with a Liminal-side address whitelist.
- The attacker got the signers to approve a transaction that [replaced the Safe's implementation](https://protos.com/a-single-malicious-transaction-led-to-230m-drained-from-wazirx/) with a malicious contract deployed eight days earlier, bypassing the multisig and the whitelist so the attacker could drain more than 200 tokens from the wallet.
- Blockchain analytics firm [Elliptic](https://www.theblock.co/post/317295/wazirx-hacker-tornado-cash) attributed the theft to North Korea's Lazarus Group, and in a January 2025 [joint statement by the United States, Japan, and South Korea](https://2021-2025.state.gov/office-of-the-spokesperson/releases/2025/01/joint-statement-on-cryptocurrency-thefts-by-the-democratic-peoples-republic-of-korea-and-public-private-collaboration/) the United States and South Korea attributed the theft to North Korea.

## Attackers

- The theft was attributed to the [Lazarus Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lazarus_Group), a North Korea-linked threat actor. Blockchain analytics firm Elliptic [said on-chain data indicated the attack was carried out by the Lazarus Group](https://www.theblock.co/post/317295/wazirx-hacker-tornado-cash).
- The primary exploiter address [`0x04b21735E93Fa3f8df70e2Da89e6922616891a88`](https://etherscan.io/address/0x04b21735e93fa3f8df70e2da89e6922616891a88) was created shortly before the attack and funded the secondary addresses [`0x35febC10112302e0d69F35F42cCe85816f8745CA`](https://etherscan.io/address/0x35febc10112302e0d69f35f42cce85816f8745ca) and [`0x90ca792206eD7Ee9bc9da0d0dF981FC5619F91Fd`](https://etherscan.io/address/0x90ca792206ed7ee9bc9da0d0df981fc5619f91fd).
- The attacker [deployed the malicious Safe implementation contract eight days before](https://cointelegraph.com/news/235m-wazirx-crypto-exchange-hack-india) the theft, indicating advance planning targeting this specific wallet.
- Stolen tokens were swapped to Ether and then sent to the sanctioned mixer [Tornado Cash](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy0916); by September 2024 the attacker had moved [more than 42,500 ETH (about $100 million)](https://www.theblock.co/post/317295/wazirx-hacker-tornado-cash) through it.
- In India, [Delhi police arrested a suspect](https://therecord.media/wazirx-crypto-exchange-hack-suspect-arrested-india), SK Masud Alam, who [opened a "mule" account under the alias Souvik Mondal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_WazirX_hack) that facilitated the hack.

## Losses

- Approximately **$230 million** in crypto assets across more than 200 tokens was drained from the affected wallet. Reported holdings included [5.43 trillion SHIB, 15,298 ETH, 640 billion PEPE, 20.5 million MATIC, 135 million GALA, and 5.79 million USDT](https://quillaudits.medium.com/another-lazarus-group-attack-decoding-wazirx-multisig-wallets-235m-exploit-8a1218a88593).
- The loss represented a large share of WazirX customer balances. WazirX [reset user balances to their July 18, 2024, 1:00 PM IST state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_WazirX_hack), undoing trades made after the breach, and [described the event as a "force majeure"](https://techcrunch.com/2024/07/21/wazirx-halts-trading-after-230-million-hit-to-crypto-exchange/).
- WazirX's Singapore parent Zettai Pte Ltd entered restructuring. On October 13, 2025, the [High Court of Singapore sanctioned a creditor-approved scheme](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2025/10/13/wazirx-restructuring-cleared-in-massive-relief-for-usd230m-hack-victims) (95.7% support by number, 94.6% by value), under which pre-hack users recover [about 85% of approved claim value in crypto and the remaining roughly 15% as non-tradable Recovery Tokens](https://www.cryptotimes.io/2026/01/10/wazirx-issues-recovery-tokens-to-users-with-locked-funds/) whose value depends on WazirX's future profits and recoveries.

## Timeline

- **Around July 10, 2024:** the attacker [deploys the malicious Safe implementation contract](https://cointelegraph.com/news/235m-wazirx-crypto-exchange-hack-india), eight days before the theft.
- **July 18, 2024:** a single transaction signed by three WazirX keys and the Liminal key [swaps the Safe implementation](https://protos.com/a-single-malicious-transaction-led-to-230m-drained-from-wazirx/) and the attacker then [drains the wallet](https://etherscan.io/tx/0x1e523051a8a481c5a57fe8261cced4cb3e775ce9315168b041fd34fc25d563ac) of about $230 million; WazirX discovers the breach and [halts all crypto and INR withdrawals](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/markets/cryptocurrency/wazirx-temporarily-suspends-crypto-deposits-and-withdrawals-after-230m-hack/articleshow/111834527.cms).
- **July 18, 2024:** the attacker [begins swapping the stolen tokens for Ether](https://crystalintelligence.com/investigations/expert-analysis-wazirx-hack/).
- **July 21, 2024:** WazirX [pauses trading and calls the loss a "force majeure" event](https://techcrunch.com/2024/07/21/wazirx-halts-trading-after-230-million-hit-to-crypto-exchange/).
- **August 14, 2024:** WazirX [ends its custody deal with Liminal and begins migrating funds to new wallets](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/markets/cryptocurrency/wazirx-ends-custody-deal-with-liminal-begins-migration-of-funds-to-new-wallets-after-230-million-hack/articleshow/112527520.cms); a [Mandiant report attributes the breach to the Liminal-side environment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_WazirX_hack), which Liminal disputes.
- **September 2024:** an independent [Grant Thornton audit finds Liminal's infrastructure was not the source](https://benzinga.com/content/40782223/liminal-custody-not-responsible-for-230-wazirx-hack-audit-finds) of the breach; Liminal and WazirX continue to dispute responsibility.
- **January 2025:** a [joint statement by the United States, Japan, and South Korea](https://2021-2025.state.gov/office-of-the-spokesperson/releases/2025/01/joint-statement-on-cryptocurrency-thefts-by-the-democratic-peoples-republic-of-korea-and-public-private-collaboration/) is published, in which the United States and South Korea attribute the WazirX theft to North Korea.
- **October 13, 2025:** the [High Court of Singapore sanctions the restructuring scheme](https://www.coindesk.com/markets/2025/10/13/wazirx-restructuring-cleared-in-massive-relief-for-usd230m-hack-victims).
- **October 24, 2025:** WazirX [resumes trading and withdrawals](https://decrypt.co/345632/wazirx-to-resume-trading-and-withdrawals-more-than-a-year-after-234m-hack), migrating custody to BitGo.
- **January 9, 2026:** WazirX [completes the first creditor distribution](https://www.cryptotimes.io/2026/01/10/wazirx-issues-recovery-tokens-to-users-with-locked-funds/), crediting about 85% of approved pre-hack balances in crypto within ten business days of the restart and issuing the remaining balance as non-tradable Recovery Tokens.

## Security Failure Causes

- **Signer deception through a manipulated custody interface:** the multisig, the address whitelist, and the Ledger hardware wallets were all in place, but the attacker used a fake Liminal interface to collect the four required signatures, starting with a [failed test USDT transfer](https://etherscan.io/tx/0x8b99ae634e1e7180b3fcc66e8fe5d076351477077051a7bbf5ec626a9d0588ef) that gathered the approvals while the wallet looked like it was operating normally. What the signers actually authorized did not match what the interface displayed, a blind-signing failure detailed in the [QuillAudits post-incident analysis](https://quillaudits.medium.com/another-lazarus-group-attack-decoding-wazirx-multisig-wallets-235m-exploit-8a1218a88593).
- **Malicious implementation swap:** the signed transaction [replaced the Gnosis Safe's implementation with an attacker-controlled contract](https://protos.com/a-single-malicious-transaction-led-to-230m-drained-from-wazirx/) deployed eight days earlier. Once the logic contract was swapped, the multisig threshold and the whitelist no longer constrained withdrawals, and the attacker drained the wallet's assets to addresses they controlled.
- **Concentration of assets in a single externally managed wallet:** a large share of customer funds sat in one custody wallet, so a single compromise of that wallet produced a near-$230 million loss.
