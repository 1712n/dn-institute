## Summary

On September 11, 2024, Indodax, Indonesia's largest cryptocurrency exchange, [was targeted in a sophisticated attack](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/1833629057256239175) that resulted in the theft of over $25 million in digital assets across multiple blockchain networks. The breach was first detected by [Cyvers, which identified suspicious transactions](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/1833629057256239175) involving Indodax wallets on different networks. The attacker compromised hot wallets on Ethereum, Bitcoin, Tron, Polygon, and Optimism chains, draining assets including ETH, BTC, USDT, MATIC, ARB, and ENA. [SlowMist's analysis](https://x.com/SlowMist_Team/status/1833757841880158673) indicated that this was not a simple hot wallet key compromise but rather a breach of the exchange's withdrawal system itself, as the attackers managed to initiate withdrawals that appeared legitimate, complete with change addresses depositing back into Indodax. The attack pattern drew comparisons to North Korea's Lazarus Group due to its speed and complexity.

## Losses

Indodax suffered a total loss of approximately $25.22 million. A [breakdown of the stolen assets](https://x.com/lookonchain/status/1833750952832368885) includes:

- *6.14M USDT*
- *1,047 ETH* (~$2.48M)
- *25 BTC* (~$1.41M)
- *2.2M MATIC* (~$849K)
- *1.4M ARB* (~$749.6K)
- *2M ENA* (~$465K)
- Additional various tokens across multiple chains

Despite the theft, the exchange's wallets continued to hold [over $400 million](https://platform.arkhamintelligence.com/explorer/entity/indodax) worth of various tokens, representing a relatively small portion of total assets under management.

## Timeline

- **September 10, 2024:** [Cyvers detected suspicious transactions](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/1833629057256239175) involving Indodax wallets on different networks, raising the first public alarm.
- **September 10, 2024:** [Over 150 suspicious transactions](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/1833635448834466252) were identified by Cyvers as part of the attack.
- **September 10-11, 2024:** [Lookonchain published a detailed breakdown](https://x.com/lookonchain/status/1833750952832368885) of the stolen assets, totaling roughly $25.22 million.
- **September 11, 2024:** [SlowMist published an analysis](https://x.com/SlowMist_Team/status/1833757841880158673) suggesting the breach went beyond a simple hot wallet compromise, pointing to a withdrawal system breach where the attackers made transactions appear legitimate.
- **September 11, 2024:** Indodax announced a "complete maintenance" to ensure system integrity, temporarily shutting down all platform operations.
- **September 11, 2024:** Indodax co-founder [William Sutanto assured users](https://x.com/WilliamSutant0/status/1833765539375874282) that their funds were "100% safe both in crypto and rupiah."
- **September 11, 2024:** The attackers swapped stolen tokens and consolidated funds across the identified attacker addresses on multiple chains.

## Security Failure Causes

**Withdrawal System Compromise:** According to [SlowMist's analysis](https://x.com/SlowMist_Team/status/1833757841880158673), the attack was not a simple hot wallet key compromise. Instead, the attackers appear to have breached the exchange's withdrawal system itself, allowing them to initiate withdrawals that looked legitimate — including change addresses depositing back into Indodax — making the attack harder to detect in real time.

**Multi-Chain Exposure:** The attack targeted hot wallets across at least six different blockchain networks simultaneously, indicating that the attackers had comprehensive access to Indodax's wallet infrastructure. This multi-chain approach allowed the attackers to maximize the amount drained before the exchange could respond.

**Delayed Detection and Response:** Despite Cyvers detecting and flagging suspicious transactions, there was a notable gap between the first external alert and Indodax's public acknowledgment and platform shutdown, during which the attackers continued to drain funds across multiple chains.
