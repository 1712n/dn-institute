## Summary

On January 8, 2026, [TrueBit, a dormant Ethereum-based protocol, was exploited for $26.2 million](https://www.theblock.co/post/384861/truebit-26-million-exploit) (8,535 ETH) through a vulnerability in its unverified bonding curve contract. The attacker minted over 240 million TRU tokens, burned them for ETH through the bonding curve, and repeated the cycle until the contract was drained. The exploit function was [literally labeled "Attack" in the transaction call](https://x.com/TheDeFiDan/status/2009299459050496249). The vulnerable contract had been [deployed approximately 5 years earlier](https://etherscan.io/address/0x764c64b2a09b09acb100b80d8c505aa6a0302ef2) with unverified bytecode and no published audits. [PeckShield traced the attacker wallet](https://x.com/PeckShieldAlert/status/2009450865942552926) to the same address that exploited Sparkle protocol for 5 ETH twelve days prior, indicating a serial attacker systematically targeting abandoned DeFi contracts. The TRU token [collapsed from $0.16 to effectively zero](https://www.coingecko.com/en/coins/truebit-protocol) following the exploit. The stolen ETH was laundered through Tornado Cash.

## Attackers

The identity of the attacker is unknown. The same wallet had previously exploited Sparkle protocol on December 27, 2025, suggesting a [serial attacker specializing in abandoned contract exploitation](https://x.com/PeckShieldAlert/status/2009450865942552926). [BlockScope traced the attacker's initial funding back to November 2025](https://x.com/BlockscopeCo/status/2009316411253116967) via Rhino.fi, indicating weeks of preparation.

## Losses

- 8,535 ETH (~$26.2 million)

## Timeline

- **~2021:** TrueBit deployed the bonding curve purchase contract on Ethereum with unverified bytecode. [Banteg flagged it as a "rug zone"](https://x.com/banteg/status/1389032239162347521) at the time.
- **November 2025:** [Attacker funded preparation wallet via Rhino.fi](https://x.com/BlockscopeCo/status/2009316411253116967).
- **December 27, 2025:** Same attacker wallet [exploited Sparkle protocol for 5 ETH](https://x.com/PeckShieldAlert/status/2009450865942552926).
- **January 8, 2026:** [Cyvers detected 8,535 ETH draining](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/2009305178277654894) from TrueBit's purchase contract.
- **January 8, 2026:** [TheDeFiDan identified](https://x.com/TheDeFiDan/status/2009299459050496249) that the exploit function was labeled "Attack" in the transaction call.
- **January 8, 2026:** Two hours later, TrueBit [acknowledged the security incident](https://x.com/Truebitprotocol/status/2009328032813850839), advising the public not to interact with the affected contract.
- **January 8, 2026:** TRU token [collapsed to near-zero](https://www.coingecko.com/en/coins/truebit-protocol). A second copycat attacker extracted approximately $250,000 in additional profits.

## Security Failure Causes

- **Unverified Bytecode:** The vulnerable contract was deployed with unverified bytecode on Ethereum, meaning its source code was never published for public review. This prevented community audits and made it impossible for users to assess the contract's security.
- **No Active Maintenance:** TrueBit had been largely dormant for years, with no active development or security monitoring on its deployed contracts. The bonding curve vulnerability existed for approximately 5 years before being exploited.
- **Overflow Vulnerability in Bonding Curve:** The attacker exploited an overflow in the token minting mechanism, allowing them to mint an excessive number of tokens and burn them for ETH in a loop. The exploit was straightforward enough that the attacker named their function "Attack."
