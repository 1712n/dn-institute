## Summary

On September 3, 2024, [Penpie](https://rekt.news/penpie-rekt/), a yield aggregator built on top of the Pendle Finance ecosystem, was exploited for approximately $27 million through a reentrancy attack. The attacker [created a fake Pendle market](https://rekt.news/penpie-rekt/) to exploit a vulnerability in Penpie's `batchHarvestMarketRewards()` function, inflating their staking balance and siphoning undeserved rewards from the protocol. [Chaofan Shou first raised the alarm](https://x.com/shoucccc/status/1831044487323529639) on Twitter, initially estimating a $17 million loss, which [Cyvers confirmed within 20 minutes](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/1831050629050954035) had grown to $27 million. The exploit was executed across both Ethereum and Arbitrum chains. [Pendle Finance confirmed](https://x.com/pendle_fi/status/1831051530214203808) its own contracts were not affected and paused all contracts as a precaution. The PNP token [dropped approximately 40%](https://rekt.news/penpie-rekt/) following the exploit, while the PENDLE token also fell about 9%. Despite having undergone [multiple audits by WatchPug, Zokyo, and AstraSec](https://docs.penpiexyz.io/security/audit-reports), the vulnerability was not caught prior to the attack.

## Timeline

- **September 3, 2024:** [Chaofan Shou raised the alarm](https://x.com/shoucccc/status/1831044487323529639) on Twitter, reporting: "Seems like Penpie got hacked. $17M loss."
- **September 3, 2024:** Within 20 minutes, [Cyvers confirmed](https://x.com/CyversAlerts/status/1831050629050954035) the attack and reported the damage had grown to $27 million.
- **September 3, 2024:** [Pendle Finance paused all contracts](https://x.com/pendle_fi/status/1831051530214203808) as a precaution and confirmed its own funds were secure.
- **September 3, 2024:** [Penpie acknowledged](https://x.com/Penpiexyz_io/status/1831058385330118831) the "security compromise" and froze all deposits and withdrawals.
- **September 3, 2024:** [PeckShield identified the root cause](https://x.com/peckshield/status/1831072098669953388) as "the introduction of an evil market that was used to inflate the staking balance to claim unwarranted rewards."
- **September 3, 2024:** [Ancilia provided technical details](https://x.com/AnciliaInc/status/1831082197312516588), explaining the exploit stemmed from a reentrancy vulnerability in Penpie's `batchHarvestMarketRewards()` function, where the attacker's contract was called during `redeemRewards()` execution.
- **September 3, 2024:** Penpie [posted a message to the hacker](https://x.com/penpiexyz_io/status/1831157212963598555) on Twitter, attempting to negotiate the return of stolen funds.
- **September 3, 2024:** [Pendle posted a post-mortem](https://x.com/pendle_fi/status/1831168623634993402) on behalf of Penpie, noting that $105 million in additional funds were protected by the contract pause.

## Security Failure Causes

- **Reentrancy Vulnerability in Reward Harvesting:** The core vulnerability was in Penpie's `batchHarvestMarketRewards()` function. When this function called `_harvestBatchMarketRewards()`, which in turn invoked `redeemRewards()` on a Pendle market, the attacker's malicious contract was able to [re-enter the function](https://x.com/AnciliaInc/status/1831082197312516588) during execution. This allowed the attacker to inflate their staking balance and claim rewards they were not entitled to — a classic reentrancy double-dip attack.

- **Lack of Market Validation:** Penpie's contracts did not verify whether a Pendle market was legitimately created by the Pendle Factory. The attacker [created a fake Pendle market](https://x.com/peckshield/status/1831072098669953388) that was accepted by Penpie's contracts as genuine, enabling the exploitation. Proper validation against Pendle's factory contract would have prevented the introduction of the malicious market.

- **Audit Gaps on Integration Points:** Despite [audits by WatchPug and Zokyo](https://docs.penpiexyz.io/security/audit-reports) in 2023, and additional audits by AstraSec in 2024, the reentrancy vulnerability in the interaction between Penpie and Pendle's market contracts was not identified. The vulnerability resided in the integration layer — the point where Penpie's contracts interacted with external Pendle market contracts — which is a common blind spot in audits focused on individual contract logic.

- **Missing Reentrancy Guards:** The exploited functions lacked proper reentrancy protection mechanisms (such as OpenZeppelin's `ReentrancyGuard`). Implementing reentrancy guards on all external-facing functions that interact with untrusted contracts would have prevented the attack.
