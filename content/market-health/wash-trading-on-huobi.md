# Uncovering Wash Trading and Market Manipulation on Huobi 🌱

Wash trading is a form of market manipulation where traders create the appearance of active trading and liquidity in a market by buying and selling the same asset among themselves. This practice can mislead other market participants into thinking that a particular asset is more active or valuable than it actually is. In this article, we will explore instances of wash trading on the Huobi cryptocurrency exchange and analyze the data to identify potential manipulation.

## Introduction to Huobi

Huobi is one of the largest cryptocurrency exchanges in the world, offering a wide range of trading pairs and services. However, like many exchanges, it has faced allegations of wash trading in the past.

## Identifying Wash Trading

To identify wash trading, we can analyze the order book and executed trades on Huobi. Specifically, we will look for patterns where large volumes of trades occur between the same set of addresses or where the bid and ask prices are very close together.

## Data Analysis

Using the [Crypto Market Health API](https://rapidapi.com/DNInstitute/api/crypto-market-health/), we collected data on trades executed on Huobi for the tokens HT, TRX, and DOGE. We then analyzed the data to identify potential instances of wash trading.

### HT Token

For the HT token, we observed a significant number of trades occurring between the same set of addresses. The bid and ask prices were also very close together, suggesting that these trades were not genuine.

### TRX Token

Similar patterns were observed for the TRX token. Large volumes of trades were executed between the same set of addresses, and the bid and ask prices were close together.

### DOGE Token

The DOGE token also showed signs of wash trading. We identified multiple instances where large volumes of trades were executed between the same set of addresses, with bid and ask prices that were very close together.

## Conclusion

Our analysis of the data collected from Huobi suggests that there may be instances of wash trading occurring on the exchange. Further investigation is needed to confirm these findings and to identify the entities responsible for the manipulation.
